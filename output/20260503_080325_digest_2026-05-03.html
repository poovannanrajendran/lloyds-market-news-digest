Palomar secures $410m of reinsurance from Torrey Pines Re 2026-1 catastrophe bond - Artemis.bm

Source: www.artemis.bm · Score: 0.78
Why it matters: Palomar's Series 2026-1 catastrophe bond provides California earthquake and Hawaii named-storm reinsurance, illustrating Lloyd's-market reinsurance/ILS activity.
  • Palomar priced Torrey Pines Re Ltd. Series 2026-1, securing $410m of capital-markets reinsurance (three California earthquake tranches + one Hawaii named‑storm tranche) on an indemnity, per‑occurrence basis for a three‑year term from June 1, 2026 to June 2029.
  • The deal was upsized from an initial $375m target to $410m and most tranches priced at or below the low end of guidance, signaling strong investor demand for the sponsor.
  • Tranche details: Class A $160m (initial expected loss 1.19%, spread 3.00%); Class B $100m (EL 2.04%, spread 3.75%); Class C $100m (EL 4.03%, spread 6.00%); Class D $50m — Hawaii (EL 0.96%, spread 2.75%).
  • By replacing $275m of maturing earthquake cover, Palomar will have $1.28bn of cat‑bond protection in force (up from $1.145bn), its largest ILS programme to date; this is Palomar’s seventh cat bond sponsorship (six consecutive years).

Somalia

Source: newsnow.co.uk · Score: 0.72
Why it matters: Discusses security, governance and humanitarian risk factors in Somalia relevant to insurance and market risk analyses.
  • Persistent high security risk from Al-Shabaab: prolonged insurgency controls rural areas and conducts urban attacks despite AU-supported operations, raising terrorism, political violence and kidnap-and-ransom exposures for assets and personnel operating in Somalia.
  • Acute humanitarian and climate-driven losses: recurring droughts, flooding and large-scale displacement create acute food insecurity and disruption to services, increasing frequency of humanitarian claims, business interruption and contingent liabilities for insurers and aid-program underwriters.
  • Fragile political and institutional environment: complex federal-regional relations, Somaliland’s unresolved status and recent state-building efforts mean elevated political-risk, contract-enforceability and sovereign/governance uncertainties for long-term investments and infrastructure underwriting.
  • Operational and maritime considerations: extensive coastline and ongoing reconstruction attract shipping, logistics and reconstruction activity — presenting marine, cargo, infrastructure and liability exposures while international stabilisation efforts can both mitigate and complicate risk landscapes.

Source: www.artemis.bm · Score: 0.72
Why it matters: Discusses AI, data centers, and ILS/capital markets—topics central to Lloyd's market dynamics.
  • AI and advanced analytics are expanding insurers' addressable markets by improving risk transparency and decision‑making—narrowing the gap between economic and insured loss and enabling more sophisticated program design and governance.
  • Digital infrastructure and AI‑driven data centre build‑outs create complex construction, operational, catastrophe and cyber exposures that exceed traditional solutions, driving new demand for tailored risk‑transfer solutions.

  • Technology adoption lets insurers and ILS tap alternative institutional capital beyond traditional insurance pools (cited move from the $4.6tn traditional pool toward a broader $250tn private/sovereign/pension capital opportunity); AI is a catalyst that amplifies existing strategy rather than a standalone strategy.
