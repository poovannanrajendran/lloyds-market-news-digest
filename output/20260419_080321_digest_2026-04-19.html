Aon estimates Q1’26 global insured catastrophe losses of $20bn at least - Artemis.bm

Source: www.artemis.bm · Score: 0.78
Why it matters: Covers catastrophe losses, perils, and market impacts relevant to Lloyd's market news.
  • Q1 2026 insured catastrophe losses totaled at least $20 billion (≈6% above the 21st‑century Q1 average) while economic losses were $37 billion—the lowest Q1 tally since 2015 and 43% below the long‑term Q1 average.
  • Losses were highly US‑concentrated: the United States accounted for >75% of global insured losses (~$16 billion), driven mainly by winter storms and severe convective storm (SCS) outbreaks; the March 10–12 SCS event caused ~$5B economic and ~$4B insured loss, and Winter Storm Fern produced ~$4.6B economic / ~$3.5B insured losses and 130+ fatalities.
  • Frequency and severity metrics above long‑term norms: 12 events caused >$1B economic losses (long‑term average 9) and five events caused >$1B insured losses (long‑term average 4); Q1 fatalities were ~1,640 (majority from flooding). Portugal recorded its costliest insured event (Windstorm Kristin, €0.9B), pushing its annual insured losses toward a new threshold when combined with flood losses.
  • Market implications—global insurance protection gap remains ~46% (kept low by heavy US insurance penetration); Aon is expanding tools (e.g., Automated Event Response for U.S. SCS) to help insurers and clients respond faster to SCS and other volatile perils.

UK's PRA to continue reforming ILS and cat bond frameworks to enhance competitiveness - Artemis.bm

Source: www.artemis.bm · Score: 0.74
Why it matters: PRA reforms to ILS/ISPV regime and catastrophe bonds in the UK; impacts Lloyd's market capital framework and access to alternative capital.
  • PRA commitment: The PRA’s 2026/27 business plan confirms continued work with HM Treasury to reform ILS and catastrophe bond frameworks to make the UK more internationally competitive.
  • ISPV approval reforms: Policy changes include an accelerated ISPV pathway (a 10‑day target for certain ILS approvals), and the PRA will begin reporting ISPV approval timelines from 2026 to speed up and clarify application/approval processes.
  • Expanding alternative capital for life insurers: A PRA discussion paper (published Nov 2025; closed early Feb) sought feedback on using ISPVs/ILS (cat bonds, sidecars, FundedRe) to bring alternative capital into life reinsurance; responses are being considered to identify regulatory changes while safeguarding policyholders.
  • Regulatory coordination and cost reduction: PRA will engage industry and work with HMT on ring‑fencing reforms and with the FCA on a proportionate captive authorisation framework and SM&CR reforms aimed at reducing costs without diluting accountability.

India Approves $1.4 Billion Maritime Insurance Pool

Source: www.insurancejournal.com · Score: 0.72
Why it matters: India's maritime insurance pool and changes in reinsurance coverage amid sanctions affect global insurance markets, including Lloyd's.
  • Government approved a ₹129.8 billion (~$1.4bn) sovereign guarantee to create a domestic maritime insurance pool running 10 years (plus a possible 5-year extension) to preserve trade continuity amid withdrawal of cover due to wars and sanctions.
  • Pool will cover all maritime risks — hull & machinery, cargo and war risk — with member insurers issuing policies using a combined underwriting capacity of about ₹9.5 billion.
  • Several major reinsurers, including state-backed GIC Re, have withdrawn cover or sharply raised premiums (citing the Iran war and Western sanctions on Russia), reducing private reinsurance support and prompting state intervention.
  • Practical implications for insurers: a sovereign backstop may restore availability of cover but will affect pricing, risk-retention and reinsurance strategies, capital allocation and potential moral‑hazard considerations — insurers should review pool terms, premium-setting and integration with existing risk-management frameworks.
