Executive summary

The Lloyd's market continues to evolve with the launch of new syndicates, sidecars, and insurance-linked securities (ILS) structures, providing opportunities for investors and reinsurers to access the market. Recent developments include the launch of Atrium's Syndicate 2026, OAK Enterprise's retrocession-focused Syndicate 1440, and Allianz's sidecar-style syndicate with Blackstone and Amwins. The Lloyd's market is experiencing significant growth, with the (Re)Insurance Specialty Index (RISX) delivering a 22.3% net total return in 2025. London Bridge 2 PCC has channeled $660m of new capital into the marketplace, and the market is expected to deliver a return on capital of around 20% for full-year 2025. The Lloyd's market and global specialty insurance sector are experiencing significant developments, with a focus on regulatory updates, market trends, and technological advancements. The Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) has issued warnings against unauthorized firms, and the establishment of a bond consolidated tape provider is underway. The Lloyd's market is experiencing significant changes with the emergence of new players, technologies, and innovations. Global specialty brokers and syndicates are adapting to these changes by leveraging digital placement platforms to enhance their operations and services. The Lloyd's market and global specialty insurance sector are undergoing significant changes driven by technological advancements, shifting consumer demands, and evolving regulatory requirements. Key trends include the increasing adoption of artificial intelligence, the growth of digital trading platforms, and the need for sector-wide skills updates.
