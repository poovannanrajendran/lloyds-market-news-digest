Lloyd's Market Executive Digest

2026-01-28 · Executive Briefing

Executive summary

The specialty insurance and reinsurance landscape is characterized by robust growth in insurance-linked securities (ILS), with catastrophe bond issuance exceeding $20 billion in 2025 and strong double-digit returns attracting sustained institutional capital. Parametric risk transfer is expanding into new frontiers such as data centres and sovereign flood risk, driven by innovation and supportive regulatory frameworks like Bermuda's proposed Parametric Special Purpose Insurer. Strategic moves, including Zurich's pursuit of Beazley, highlight the convergence of traditional and alternative capital and the strategic value of specialty underwriters. Brokers and syndicates remain pivotal in facilitating efficient placement and capital discipline, ensuring the market's resilience and capacity to meet evolving global risk demands.

  • Strategic Corporate Actions and Market Consolidation
