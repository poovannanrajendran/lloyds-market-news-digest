Based on the provided information from the Artemis website, here are some key points and updates related to Lloyd's of London:

1. Increasing use of third-party capital: There is a growing trend of re/insurers partnering with investment managers or private equity firms to launch special purpose vehicles (SPVs) or sidecar-style syndicates at Lloyd's. Examples include:
   - Allianz and Oaktree Capital Management launching Syndicate 1890
   - AIG setting up Syndicate 2479 in partnership with Blackstone, Amwins, and Palantir AI

2. London Bridge 2 PCC gaining traction: This insurance-linked securities (ILS) structure is being used increasingly to channel third-party capital into Lloyd's:
   - Al Wathba Insurance became the first insurer from the UAE to use London Bridge 2 to allocate capital to Lloyd's opportunities
   - In Q4 2025, London Bridge 2 opened 14 cells and channeled $660 million of new Lloyd's capital

3. Growth in U.S. market: More than half of Lloyd's business now originates from North America, primarily the United States.

4. New syndicates launched:
   - OAK Global launching Syndicate 1440 focused on retrocession
   - AIG and Blackstone's Syndicate 2479

5. Investor interest: Argenta Private Capital Limited (APCL) highlights investing in Lloyd's as a logical and underutilized pathway to returns.

6. Diversification opportunities: Re/insurers are using structures like London Bridge 2 PCC to access new sources of diversification for their businesses.

7. Technology integration: Palantir's AI is being used by AIG in its latest SPV at Lloyd's, demonstrating the market's embrace of technological innovation.

These developments indicate a growing convergence between traditional re/insurance and alternative capital markets through the use of innovative structures like London Bridge 2 PCC.
