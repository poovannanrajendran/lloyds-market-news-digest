Global credit insurance market supports AFDB in Zambia’s buy-back transaction - Reinsurance News

Source: www.reinsurancene.ws · Score: 0.82
Why it matters: Describes private credit insurance and insurer involvement in AfDB-backed Zambia debt-financing.
  • Zambia repurchased its $1.36bn sovereign Eurobond using domestic funds plus a $600m AfDB loan, with private credit insurers providing bespoke risk capacity that enabled the buyback and de-risked the AfDB-led package.
  • The deal demonstrates the concrete role credit insurance and political-risk capacity can play in mobilising risk-bearing private capital, crowding in multilateral lending, and amplifying a development bank’s balance sheet to support sovereign liability management.
  • Structurally, the transaction freed about $275m in annual debt‑servicing savings earmarked for energy investment (debt‑for‑energy), illustrating how insurer-backed solutions can translate into measurable development outcomes and new product use-cases for the market.
  • Implications for insurers: expanded business and reputational opportunities in development finance, but greater need for cross‑sector structuring expertise, sovereign risk assessment, capacity allocation and coordination with multilaterals—plus careful governance on pricing, concentration and enforceability when underwriting similar sovereign solutions.

UK businesses confident about trade credit resilience – but research results show different conclusion

Source: www.insurancetimes.co.uk · Score: 0.72
Why it matters: Covers trade credit risk and insurance implications relevant to Lloyd's market.
  • Large perception gap: 98% of surveyed UK CEOs/CFOs believe they are resilient, yet 85% report late payments and 75% have written off bad debts; 25% say up to 50% of revenue would be at risk if a top‑5 customer failed — indicating material underestimation of counterparty/default risk.
  • Rising concentration and optimism: 99% of firms now rely on smaller supply chains than in 2025 while 84% expect growth in 2026, increasing supplier/customer concentration and portfolio vulnerability for insurers.
  • Actionable risk management need: Marsh urges firms to align confidence with visibility via better risk analytics, ongoing exposure monitoring and regular reviews — an area where insurers/brokers can provide data-driven services and advisory.
  • Trade credit insurance as an enabler: Marsh highlights TCI as a practical mitigation to support growth strategies and board-level appetite for new markets or partnerships — a sales and risk-transfer opportunity for carriers and brokers.
