California appeals court says exclusivity bars asbestos tort claims - Business Insurance

Source: www.businessinsurance.com · Score: 0.65
Why it matters: Discusses exclusivity of workers' comp for asbestos exposure claims and related insurance implications.
  • California’s 4th District Court of Appeal (unpublished decision in Alina Cadena et al. v. City of San Diego et al.) held that workers’ compensation is the exclusive remedy for employees’ asbestos exposure claims against the city and a city official, affirming summary judgment for the city.
  • The court rejected the fraudulent-concealment exception because plaintiffs could not show the employer had actual knowledge of the injury/exposure before the employees did; without that prior knowledge, concealment cannot be established.
  • The court also confirmed that claims for intentional infliction of emotional distress (and related punitive damages) are barred by workers’ compensation exclusivity under California precedent.
  • Practical implications for insurers: this decision limits tort exposure for municipal/employer defendants in similar asbestos scenarios, highlights the importance of contemporaneous records and timely disclosure to defend against concealment allegations, and affects allocation of potential liability to workers’ compensation rather than general liability/punitive exposures.
