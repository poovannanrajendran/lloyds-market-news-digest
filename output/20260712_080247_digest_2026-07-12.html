Insurance capacity constraints for data centres to drive ILS use: S&P - Artemis.bm

Source: www.artemis.bm · Score: 0.85
Why it matters: Discusses capacity constraints for hyperscale data center insurance and use of captives/ILS—key topics for Lloyd's market.
  • Large market opportunity and scale: S&P estimates rising data‑centre insurance demand could generate about $10bn of new premiums in 2026, with global data‑centre investment potentially exceeding $300bn by 2027; individual hyperscale sites may have construction insurable values of $10bn–$30bn (well above typical $5bn–$10bn limits).
  • Broader and material exposures beyond bricks and mortar: IT hardware, critical infrastructure and operational risks (business interruption from downtime, power dependency, tech failures) can represent a material or greater share of total insurable value compared with physical construction losses.
  • Capacity limits and partial self‑insurance: As total insurable values approach $20bn–$30bn per location, traditional insurers are unlikely to fully insure hyperscale projects, meaning many operational and technology risks will remain self‑insured or only partially insured.
  • Evolving risk-transfer solutions: Expect greater use of captives and other self‑insurance vehicles, more collaborative multi‑insurer/reinsurer placement structures to pool capacity, and growing involvement from alternative capital (eg, ILS) to help close the coverage gap.

Existing cyber cat bonds structured to withstand Claude Mythos turbulence: Man Group - Artemis.bm

Source: www.artemis.bm · Score: 0.75
Why it matters: Discusses cyber catastrophe bonds (cat bonds) and ILS market dynamics impacting insurers, a Lloyd's market topic.
  • Anthropic’s Mythos Preview shows advanced capabilities (autonomously finding/exploiting zero‑days and reverse‑engineering), raising the cyber threat by enabling scaled attacks and lowering the skills needed by malicious actors.
  • Man Group argues existing cyber catastrophe bonds—predominantly per‑occurrence structures with high attachment points—are likely insulated in the near term because payouts require single events causing losses well above historical levels.
  • Two core challenges for insurers/ILS: how to legally and operationally aggregate linked cyber incidents (is a simultaneous AI‑driven attack one event?) and whether AI outages deserve sub‑limits similar to cloud service outages.
  • Market reaction has been muted so far (stable pricing, no sell‑off), but Man Group warns that Mythos accelerates vulnerability discovery and resets the maturity clock on cyber risk models, increasing near‑term uncertainty.

Porch secures debut $100m Harbor Crest Re 2026-1 catastrophe bond at lower pricing - Artemis.bm

Source: www.artemis.bm · Score: 0.72
Why it matters: Details a US catastrophe bond deal and pricing (reinsurance via ILS), which informs Lloyd's market on capital-market risk transfer activity.
  • Porch Group launched its debut catastrophe bond via Harbor Crest Re Ltd. (Series 2026-1): a single $100m Class A tranche providing roughly four years of collateralized, multi-peril reinsurance to Porch Insurance Reciprocal Exchange and Homeowners of America.
  • Coverage is per-occurrence and indemnity-triggered, protecting against named storms, winter storms, severe weather, wildfire and fire-following earthquake across all 50 US states and D.C.; initial base expected loss for the tranche is 1.97%.
  • The notes priced at a 4.5% spread—at the bottom of the reduced guidance (4.5%–5%), and below initial guidance—so Porch secured its targeted $100m limit at lower-than-anticipated cost.
  • The deal underscores the broader market trend of first-time sponsors accessing the cat bond market for efficient, diversified reinsurance capacity.
