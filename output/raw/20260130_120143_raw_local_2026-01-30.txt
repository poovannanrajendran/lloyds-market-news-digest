Here are some key insights from the recent articles on The Insurer and related sites:

- Willis Re's Cameron Rye said the year-on-year reduction in 2025 catastrophe losses does not indicate lower risk, but rather reflects luck. He warned against complacency.

- Axis Capital expects mid-to-high single digit growth for its insurance business in 2026 while reinsurance volume may decline. They are shifting towards delegated underwriting with MGUs and third-party capital, which now accounts for about 32% of their insurance portfolio.

- There has been an uptick in cyberattacks recently, hitting companies like Bumble, Match Group, Panera Bread and CrunchBase according to Bloomberg News reports.

- The parametric insurance market is shifting from a niche byproduct to a core tool as climate-related losses increase, according to Descartes' Guillaume Vetter. Parametrics are expected to grow in importance for risk transfer.

- The U.S. E&S property market has seen increased submissions despite softening rates due to more frequent claims activity and limited capacity in the primary market.

- There is commentary suggesting a potential stabilization of the U.S. casualty insurance market after several years of hardening conditions.

- Guillaume Bonnissent advises insurers to "keep up" with rapid technology advancements, noting that AI will play an increasingly important role going forward.

The articles highlight shifting trends in re/insurance markets and emerging risks like cyberattacks and climate change impacts, while also emphasizing the growing importance of technology and innovation for the industry.
