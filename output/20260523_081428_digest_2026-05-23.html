NOAA forecasts 55% chance of below-normal Atlantic hurricane season, as El Nino nears - Artemis.bm

Source: www.artemis.bm · Score: 0.84
Why it matters: NOAA hurricane forecast and El Niño-driven risk implications for insurance/ILS markets relevant to Lloyd's.
  • NOAA’s first 2026 Atlantic forecast: 8–14 named storms, 3–6 hurricanes, 1–3 major hurricanes (Category 3+); 55% chance of a below‑normal season, 35% near‑normal, 10% above‑normal, with ~70% confidence in these ranges.
  • El Niño expected to develop (82% chance May–July) and is the primary reason for the below‑normal outlook — it tends to suppress Atlantic storm formation via increased wind shear, but warmer-than-normal Atlantic SSTs and weaker trade winds could offset some suppression.
  • Critical caveat for insurers/reinsurers/ILS investors: a below‑normal season does not eliminate risk — jet‑stream shifts during El Niño can steer storms toward the mid‑Atlantic increasing storm‑surge and landfall risk, and one major landfalling event can drive large claims and market dislocation.
  • Practical implications: maintain and test hurricane preparedness plans, review cat‑bond/ILS and reinsurance portfolio construction and concentrations, and avoid overreacting to seasonal counts — prudent exposure management and diversification remain the dominant risk controls.
