London Market signal: Counterparty fraud, third‑country capital rules and shifting ILS flows are reshaping how we onboard, place and price specialist risks.

Why it matters: These are operational realities — tighten onboarding/payment controls, revisit entity strategy, and adapt packaging and analytics now to protect premiums, capacity and placements.

1) FCA warning on HIVE‑TRADE unauthorised firm - Why it matters: Unauthorised counterparties can misdirect premiums or collateral, enable mis‑selling and leave brokers/syndicates without Financial Ombudsman/FSCS recourse.
2) PRA PS13/26 (third‑country branches; £600m subsidiarisation threshold) - Why it matters: Forces reassessment of branch vs subsidiary structures, with direct impacts on capital allocation, solvency modelling and cross‑border placement workflows.
3) Investors favour scalable ILS structures as allocations rise - Why it matters: Growing institutional demand pressures syndicates, MGAs and platforms to standardise ILS product features, reporting and investor‑facing documentation to access capital.
4) Surge in strikes, riots and civil commotion (SRCC) claims - Why it matters: Raises correlation risk across property, political risk and BI lines, requiring tighter wording and real‑time exposure analytics to avoid aggregation losses.
