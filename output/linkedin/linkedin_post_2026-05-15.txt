Signal from the London Market: Regulatory warnings and rapid tech adoption are tightening how capacity is placed, documented and paid for.

FCA action, senior broker hires and AI contract tools in this digest mean brokers, syndicates and platforms must prioritise counterparty checks, contract intelligence and ILS-aware placement strategies now.

1) FCA warning — Ourbit (www.ourbit.com) - Why it matters: The FCA warning highlights unauthorised digital operators that can impersonate counterparties, so brokers and platforms must verify counterparties and harden onboarding to avoid fraud, settlement and reputational risk.
2) Northern Re expands Ultrassure platform - Why it matters: Production deployment of Ultrassure shows AI contract intelligence turning complex reinsurance docs into searchable, auditable data, speeding contract ops and reducing disputes.
3) Aon adds Mitchell and Rimmer to Global ReSpecialty team - Why it matters: These hires materially strengthen Aon’s proportional reinsurance capability, likely increasing proportional submission volumes and competition for mandates into Lloyd’s and global reinsurers.
4) Mangrove targets up to $111m named‑storm protection via Buttonwood Re cat bond - Why it matters: A $111m cat‑bond pursuit signals growing ILS competition for peak peril capacity, requiring syndicates and brokers to integrate ILS into placement and capital strategies.
