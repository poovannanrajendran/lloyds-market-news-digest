Regulators and platforms are forcing a sharp reprioritisation across the London Market — boards need action plans now on capital, custody and data flows.

Why it matters: Bank of England/PRA signals plus new trading and submission tools mean syndicates, brokers and placement platforms must sequence governance, systems and capital work to avoid operational or prudential surprises.

1) Tenth edition of the Bank of England Regulatory Initiatives Grid - Why it matters: Provides a near‑term consolidated timetable so syndicates, brokers and platforms can sequence compliance, systems and governance investments to align with PRA/FCA timelines.  
2) PRA letter on prudential treatment of tokenised assets and stablecoins - Why it matters: Sets supervisory expectations that require syndicates to reassess capital models, custody arrangements and reporting for tokenised holdings and crypto counterparty exposures.  
3) Fidelis Partnership and Howden launch automated broker submission process (Broker Connect) - Why it matters: Embeds ACORD ADEPT structured submissions into underwriter workflows, reducing manual rekeying, improving data accuracy and shortening time‑to‑bind on complex lead‑led placements.  
4) Aon to launch digital trading platform for follow‑line placements (Aon DPX) - Why it matters: Seeks to create a structured, algorithmic trading venue for follow business that could reshape access to capital, pricing dynamics and governance for follow‑line placements.
