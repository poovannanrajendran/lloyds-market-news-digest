London Market signal: Alternative capital and placement channels are materially changing capacity, pricing and distribution.

Why it matters: ILS and cat bonds are increasingly competing with traditional reinsurance as rates soften, while regulatory and channel‑risks mean brokers, syndicates and platforms must sharpen onboarding, analytics and placement workflows.

1) Palomar’s $410m Torrey Pines cat bond - Why it matters: Underscores sponsor appetite to shift concentrated US nat‑cat layers into capital markets, increasing competition for high‑severity capacity.  
2) Slide expanding to a ≈$3.5bn reinsurance tower with substantial rate decreases - Why it matters: Growing aggregate catastrophe limit but softer pricing pressures underwriting discipline and portfolio reallocation.  
3) Swiss Re Sigma: Asian cat losses hit $65bn - Why it matters: Exposes a large protection gap and creates commercial demand for affordable parametric/index solutions and local distribution models.  
4) FCA warning on Cenor Holdings (unauthorised firm) - Why it matters: Highlights channel contamination risk and the need for immediate tightening of digital/referral onboarding and monitoring to protect reputation and compliance.
