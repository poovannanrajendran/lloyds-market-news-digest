Signal for the London Market: Crypto regulation and product innovation are shifting from experiment to infrastructure.

Why it matters: The Bank of England–FCA joint approach to systemic stablecoins and the FCA’s landmark crypto rulebook raise prudential, market‑integrity and resilience expectations for platforms, custodians and anyone using tokenised rails — while new products and capital flows (Demex, KKR) are changing capacity and structuring dynamics.

1) Joint FCA–Bank of England supervision of systemic stablecoin issuers - Why it matters: Creates higher prudential oversight and potential resolution tools for payment/settlement rails used for premiums, collateral and tokenised custody, requiring syndicates and platforms to revise due diligence and contingency plans.
2) FCA landmark crypto rules - Why it matters: Raises capital, conduct and resilience standards for exchanges, custodians and platforms, forcing reassessment of counterparty strength, onboarding timelines and platform certification used by Lloyd’s participants.
3) Demex modelled‑loss aggregate winter storm product - Why it matters: Parametric/modelled triggers can speed pay‑outs and deterministic reinstatement of capacity, but syndicates must validate models and manage basis‑risk before deployment.
4) KKR elevating reinsurance in capital market assumptions - Why it matters: Signals growing institutional allocation to reinsurance that will influence supply, pricing and demand for standardised reporting and investor‑friendly deal structures from syndicates and brokers.
