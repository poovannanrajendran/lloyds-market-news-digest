Signal from the London Market: Regulatory scrutiny and shifting risk drivers are changing how we place, route and price specialty business.

Why it matters: If you work in placement, underwriting or platform ops, these items affect counterparty screening, premium flows, capacity planning and marine/war-risk pricing — act now to protect onboarding, claims and reinsurance strategies.

1) Northbank Private Banking (FCA warning) - Why it matters: Appearing on the FCA warning list creates counterparty, payment-routing and reputational risk for brokers, placement platforms and syndicates if clients or counterparties use it to move premium or claims funds.
2) Regulators propose UK captive insurance regime - Why it matters: The PRA/FCA proposal for a tailored UK captive regime could shift domicile choices, reduce ceded premium to traditional reinsurers and change syndicate appetite and retention strategies.
3) Storms cause more than $700M in insured losses - Why it matters: Large storm losses underscore persistent catastrophe exposure and will prompt syndicates and brokers to reassess modelling, aggregation and reinsurance structures.
4) India bars seafarers from Hormuz voyages after attacks - Why it matters: The crew ban materially affects marine crew availability, war/P&I exposures and routing decisions, likely driving higher war-risk premiums and reinsurance demand.
