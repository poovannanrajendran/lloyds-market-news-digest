Mid‑year signal for the London Market: Property and marine pricing are softening even as litigation and long‑tail exposures keep claims and reserving under pressure.

Why it matters: Underwriters, brokers and capital providers face diverging pressures at renewals — cheaper capacity and ILS expansion in some lines, versus heightened regulatory and litigation-driven scrutiny in others.

2) Property reinsurance softening (Guy Carpenter) - Why it matters: Abundant capacity and ILS growth are accelerating rate softening, pushing buyers toward parametric and sidecar solutions and pressuring syndicate pricing and aggregate appetites.
3) FCA removes non‑UK business from Consumer Duty scope - Why it matters: Firms transacting cross‑border now face changed conduct obligations, requiring updates to onboarding, documentation and compliance resource allocation across brokers and syndicates.
4) Hormuz transit drops after vessel attacks - Why it matters: Reduced Gulf traffic raises demand and repricing pressure for war/terror and kidnap/political violence covers and forces routing and terms changes for marine underwriters.
