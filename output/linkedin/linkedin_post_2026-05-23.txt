Signal from the London Market: This morning’s digest flags shifting capital, distribution and counterparty risks that should change how we place and price specialty business.

If you work in syndicates, managing agents, broking or placement platforms, four items stand out that should shape immediate underwriting and operational priorities.

1) Money Helpline (FCA warning) - Why it matters: An unauthorised promoter can lead to premium diversion and uninsured losses, exposing brokers, syndicates and platforms to claims disputes and reputational harm.
2) MS Amlin profit up £92m in 2025 - Why it matters: Stronger insurance-service results point to improved carrier economics and potential greater capacity availability that may moderate hardening in select specialty classes.
3) CIAB: Premiums falling across all accounts - Why it matters: A softening commercial market pressures combined ratios and forces syndicates to tighten underwriting discipline while brokers pivot to value‑added services.
4) ILS/non‑traditional capital as a "critical extension" - Why it matters: Treating ILS as core capital requires syndicates, placement platforms and brokers to integrate ILS into capital modelling, product design and investor-facing structuring to access hybrid capacity.
