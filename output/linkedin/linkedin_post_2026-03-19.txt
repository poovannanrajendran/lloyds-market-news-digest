Heads-up for the London Market: Tightening incident/third‑party rules plus data‑reporting and regional expansion signals mean this quarter is about harder compliance and platform readiness.

Why it matters: FCA/PRA policy moves and DRSP authorisation requirements increase notification, vendor‑governance and data‑flow obligations that will directly affect syndicates, brokers and placement platforms—start with third‑party inventories and DRSP checks.

1) FCA confirms new incident and third‑party rules - Why it matters: Raises reporting frequency and specificity expectations, increasing compliance risk and the need for faster, co‑ordinated incident response across syndicates, brokers and platforms.
2) Data Reporting Service Providers (ARMs/APAs/CTPs) authorisation requirements - Why it matters: Brokers, syndicates and platforms must verify provider authorisation and validate end‑to‑end data flows and reconciliation to meet FCA standards.
3) Lloyd’s reported application to set up in India’s GIFT City - Why it matters: A regional Lloyd’s domicile/platform could ease placements for Indian exposures and require platform integration and tax/reporting assessment by managing agents.
4) PS7/26 – PRA final policy on operational incident and third‑party reporting - Why it matters: Mandates inventorying and risk‑ranking material third‑party providers, updated contracts and proportionate MTP governance to satisfy PRA notification expectations.
