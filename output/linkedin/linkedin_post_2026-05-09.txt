London Market signal: Brokers are becoming the primary bridge to institutional capital while regulatory warnings and insurtech capacity shifts force tougher placement hygiene.

This matters for syndicates, brokers and placement platforms because capital origination, AML screening and vendor integration will directly change how we price, document and deliver placements.

1) Howden Re appoints Tim Radford as Managing Director, Head of Lloyd's Capital - Why it matters: Reinforces broker-led capital origination and accelerates institutional/ILS access to Lloyd's, altering syndicate funding dynamics and partner selection.
2) FCA warning on Edgebitpro (FCA Warning List) - Why it matters: Highlights unauthorised-operator risk that exposes brokers, platforms and syndicates to fraud, AML vulnerabilities and client redress unless counterparty screening is tightened.
3) Allianz moves commercial cyber portfolio to Coalition - Why it matters: Shows capacity and capability shifting to an insurtech partner, forcing brokers and syndicates to reassess cyber capacity, pricing and API/integration requirements.
4) FEMA Review Council recommends NFIP reform with bigger private-market role - Why it matters: Expands the addressable US flood market, requiring syndicates and brokers to establish standardised underwriting, pricing governance and multi-carrier placement workflows.
