Signal from the London Market: Capacity stress, alternative capital and reputational pressure are converging.

Why it matters: Today’s digest highlights concentrated aggregation risks, faster-moving alternative capacity and rising public activism — all of which change underwriting appetite, placement workflows and capital planning.

3) H1 2026 catastrophe bond issuance $16.1bn (144A $15.8bn) - Why it matters: Near‑record cat bond volumes expand alternative reinsurance capacity and alter capital planning and hedging strategies for primary insurers and Lloyd’s syndicates.
4) Protests at Lloyd’s and the Gherkin over Coral Triangle gas projects - Why it matters: Public activism targeting fossil fuel projects raises reputational and underwriting‑policy pressure that can prompt stricter LNG/upstream exposure guidance.
