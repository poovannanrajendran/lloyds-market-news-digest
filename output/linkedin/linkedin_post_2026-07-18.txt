London Market signal: Regulatory reform, senior hires and AI litigation are already reshaping where capacity flows and how risks get placed.

Recent digest items point to concentrated opportunities — captives, ceded strategy and broker competition — and rising AI-related litigation exposure that underwriters and platforms need to address now.

1) Regulators propose UK captive insurance regime - Why it matters: A PRA/FCA proposal targeting a four-to-six week authorisation will drive captive formation and reinsurance-placement work while altering retained-risk strategies and ceded-reinsurance demand.  
2) IMA hires two former Lockton brokers for its global practice - Why it matters: The lateral hires strengthen IMA’s global practice and can redirect specialty placement volumes, intensifying competition for Lloyd’s placements.  
3) QBE appoints head of outward reinsurance (from Munich Re) - Why it matters: Centralising ceded-reinsurance strategy in London is likely to streamline procurement and shift treaty/facultative demand, requiring brokers and platforms to adjust workflows and analytics.  
4) Judge won’t block Meta layoffs tied to an AI-bias suit - Why it matters: The ruling highlights growing litigation linked to AI deployment, increasing demand for clearer AI governance in D&O/EPLI/cyber underwriting and tailored endorsements.
