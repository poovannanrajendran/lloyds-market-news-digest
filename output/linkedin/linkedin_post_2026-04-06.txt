London Market signal: Softening reinsurance rates are colliding with a clear rise in alternative capital — placement playbooks need updating.

Why it matters: This digest shows concrete shifts — Aon pegs ILS at $136bn (+18% in 2025), Guy Carpenter reports Asia/India treaty rates down >20% at Apr 1, and non‑traditional capital is being deployed into tech and specialty risks — all of which change pricing power, capacity sourcing and concentration management for syndicates, brokers and platforms. Read the full digest: https://poovannanrajendran.github.io/lloyds-market-news-digest/digests/digest_2026-04-06.html

1) Alternative/ILS capital grew 18% to $136bn in 2025 (Aon) - Why it matters: Elevated ILS capacity increases third‑party supply, forcing brokers and syndicates to integrate ILS into placements and manage model and concentration risk.

2) Asia/India reinsurance rates fell >20% at Apr 1 renewals (Guy Carpenter) - Why it matters: Material rate compression on Asian business directly pressures top‑line premium and necessitates treaty and underwriting repricing to protect margins.

3) Insurers are turning to hedge funds to cover data centre risks (Business Insurance) - Why it matters: Hedge‑fund-backed structures create bespoke capital options that require extra due diligence on governance, triggers and counterparty settlement certainty.

4) Marine reinsurance remains soft despite war (Gallagher Re) - Why it matters: Ongoing softness in marine rates sustains competitive pressure on hull, cargo and war covers, so syndicates must differentiate on risk selection and service to defend margins.
