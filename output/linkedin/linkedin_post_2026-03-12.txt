Signal for the London Market: Payments fragility and alternative capital are shaping underwriting and operational priorities this week.

Why it matters: Syndicates, brokers, MGAs and placement platforms need to shore up payment and contingency plans while recalibrating capital and peak-layer sourcing to respond to geopolitical and catastrophe risk shifts.

1) FCA restrictions on Sendsii Ltd (PSP) - Why it matters: Suspension of a payment provider can disrupt premium collection and claims disbursement, so firms must have contingency playbooks that can be enacted within 24–72 hours.  
2) Reinsurance sidecar market up ~183% since 2023 to about $16–18bn (AM Best) - Why it matters: Rapid sidecar growth expands institutional capital options and requires clear governance, transparent structures and tailored structuring capabilities from syndicates and MGAs.  
3) Multiple ships struck in the Strait of Hormuz - Why it matters: The attacks harden war-risk, hull and cargo pricing and force reassessment of exposure concentrations and routing for MENA transit corridors.  
4) The Hartford again uses catastrophe bonds to extend its reinsurance tower - Why it matters: Repeated cat‑bond issuance reaffirms cat bonds as efficient peak-layer capacity, signalling a need to scale origination and structuring capabilities.
