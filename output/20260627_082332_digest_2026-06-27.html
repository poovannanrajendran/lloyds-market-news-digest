Microstructure, not correlation, shields pure-play cat bond ETFs from deleveraging regimes: King Ridge’s Pagnani - Artemis.bm

Source: www.artemis.bm · Score: 0.82
Why it matters: Discusses catastrophe bond ETFs, ILS, and market-stress dynamics directly relevant to Lloyd's market.
  • Cat-bond ETFs are structurally different from leveraged high‑yield credit: when markets are stressed, the absence of leverage, broad diversification and contractual transparency have historically produced orderly price adjustments rather than indiscriminate forced selling.
  • ETF mechanics reduce balance-sheet driven liquidity risk: these funds invest only in publicly issued catastrophe bonds (excluding sidecars/quota shares), operate without margin/financing, and can meet outflows via in‑kind redemptions to avoid selling into stressed secondary markets.
  • Return drivers and loss profile are stabilising: contractual coupon carry provides steady income, realised losses are typically discrete and bounded, and diversification across peril, geography and attachment points localises event risk to affected bonds rather than propagating across the portfolio.
  • Transparency and market microstructure support resilience: standardised documentation, observable triggers/attachment points and fully funded collateral let participants calculate exposures mid‑crisis, reducing adverse selection and enabling more continuous price discovery; bid‑ask widening tends to be asymmetric and targeted to affected issues.
