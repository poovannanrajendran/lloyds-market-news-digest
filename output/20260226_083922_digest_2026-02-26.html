Allstate secures $1.2bn reinsurance with new Sanders Re cat bonds, its largest placement ever - Artemis.bm

Source: www.artemis.bm · Score: 0.82
Why it matters: Catastrophe bond issuance and $1.2B reinsurance capacity by Allstate; relevant to re/insurance markets including Lloyd's.
  • Allstate priced $1.2 billion of multi-peril, multi-year catastrophe bonds via Sanders Re IV (Series 2026‑1A) and Sanders Re III (Series 2026‑1B) — the largest amount of reinsurance limit the insurer has ever secured in a single market visit (upsized from an initial ~$500m target).

  • Strategic implications: the issuance will raise Allstate’s cat‑bond capacity (outstanding to roughly $3.8bn after April maturities), reinforces its top sponsor position, and demonstrates the ILS market’s ability to provide competitively priced, multi‑year (4–5 year) reinsurance capacity for large insurers seeking longer‑duration certainty.

Ransomware sublimit doesn’t apply to cyber claim: Court - Business Insurance

Source: www.businessinsurance.com · Score: 0.71
Why it matters: Clarifies ransomware sublimit application in cyber policies; may influence Lloyd's cyber underwriting and pricing.
  • A federal court in Dallas held that HSB Specialty Insurance’s $250,000 ransomware sublimit endorsement did not apply to CiCi Enterprises’ ransomware loss because the endorsement’s language was ambiguous and did not clearly state it modified the policy’s cyber extortion coverage.
  • CiCi reported more than $1.2 million in losses from a May 2022 ransomware attack; the underlying policy had a $3 million overall limit, but HSB sought to cap related losses at $250,000 via the endorsement.
  • Under Texas law, unclear limiting provisions are construed against the insurer, and the court found HSB failed to expressly state the sublimit would apply “across the board,” allowing the claim to proceed.
  • Practical implications for insurers, MGAs and brokers: endorsements must explicitly identify which insuring agreements they alter and how sublimits apply; precise drafting and placement of cyber endorsements is critical to avoid coverage disputes.
