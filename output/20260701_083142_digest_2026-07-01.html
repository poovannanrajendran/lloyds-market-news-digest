Opinion: Analysing chokepoint exposures after the Strait of Hormuz crisis

Source: www.globalreinsurance.com · Score: 0.82
Why it matters: Discusses Hormuz disruption and its implications for re/insurance pricing, exposure, and Lloyd's Joint War Committee decisions.
  • 100+ day Strait of Hormuz closure produced large direct insured losses (vessels trapped, 14 seafarer deaths, damage to Ras Laffan) and indirect impacts across cargo, energy and commodity markets; Howden Re estimates $2–3bn claims—exceeding the entire annual global premium base for the war/terror/political violence class.
  • The event exposed critical accumulation risk: Lloyd’s Joint War Committee expanded listed areas and underwriters were forced to reprice or restrict exposure as the market proved unable to absorb a single-corridor $2–3bn shock without structural change.
  • Supply‑chain fragility and cascading losses were highlighted—multiple chokepoints on a single route, reliance on long‑term contracts, boil‑off/extended voyage costs, commodity and sovereign knock‑on effects—showing many losses are indirect, hard to price, and can cross multiple lines (marine, credit, property, political risk).
  • Key takeaway for insurers: chokepoints can be leveraged for months, not days, and other passages (Malacca, Panama, Suez/Bab el‑Mandeb) present distinct, concentrated exposures by cargo class and route; insurers must aggregate route/node accumulations, update modeling, monitoring (e.g., AIS spoofing/transponder silence) and rethink pricing, limits and reinsurance strategies.

Demex launches modelled-loss trigger aggregate winter storm reinsurance product - Artemis.bm

Source: www.artemis.bm · Score: 0.78
Why it matters: Introduces a modeled-loss trigger winter storm reinsurance product; discusses reinsurance market trends relevant to Lloyd's risk transfer.
  • Demex has launched a modelled-loss trigger winter-storm reinsurance product for US primary carriers to cover severe winter storms, extreme cold and heavy snowfall.
  • The product builds a cedent-specific modeled-loss index by overlaying a carrier’s ground-up claims history (past ~10 years) with corresponding weather data, incorporating socio‑economic exposure factors.
  • Reinsurers provide limits tied to the modeled-loss index; once the index exceeds the attachment point the carrier may draw on the aggregate reinsurance limit (functionally similar to indemnity aggregate cover).
  • Demex asserts the approach reduces loss-cost uncertainty for reinsurers, can produce materially lower rate-on-line versus traditional aggregate indemnity cover, and is positioned as better tailored than industry/industry-calibrated models using broader datasets.
