Africa’s investment future depends on understanding risk differently – ATIDI

Source: www.globalreinsurance.com · Score: 0.82
Why it matters: Emphasizes risk mitigation, reinsurance, and risk instruments to unlock Africa infrastructure investment; relevant to Lloyd's market.
  • Confidence, not just capital: Africa’s persistent risk premium stems from broad perceptions rather than country‑specific realities—more precise risk assessment and transparent risk allocation are critical to lower financing costs and unlock long‑term private investment.

Hyperscale data centres a growing but increasingly selective opportunity for re/insurers: S&P - Reinsurance News

Source: www.reinsurancene.ws · Score: 0.80
Why it matters: Covers insurer capacity, risk layering, aggregation, and underwriting trends for hyperscale data centres, topics pertinent to the Lloyd's market.
  • Hyperscale data‑centre campuses are driving construction‑phase insured values into the $20bn–$50bn range, creating an ‘insurance gap’ where full replacement‑style coverage at a single site is increasingly hard to secure and may constrain lender recoverability in downside scenarios.
  • Insurers are moving toward PML/MFL‑based and layered placements that may only cover part of total project value — PML assumes partial safety‑measure impairment, MFL assumes complete safeguard failure — enabling higher aggregate limits but leaving larger uninsured exposures.
  • Aggregation risk and multiphase development are key challenges: large campuses concentrate exposure at single sites and require dynamic, ‘stacked’ limits as buildings move from construction to operation, complicating accumulation management.
  • Market response is becoming more selective: tighter underwriting, higher attachment points, partial coverage, controlled BI/obsolescence exposure, and stronger emphasis on risk engineering, exposure transparency and portfolio‑level accumulation controls.

The Shifting Composition of Reliable Carriers

Source: www.insurancejournal.com · Score: 0.72
Why it matters: Covers hard market dynamics, rate inflation, carrier performance, and competitive shifts—contextual to Lloyd's market news.
  • Context and scale: industry faced the toughest market in 40 years—premiums up ~40% over five years and carriers reporting record net income (~$398.3B for three straight years)—yet a meaningful minority of major carriers shrank (4% of top 90 had two-year negative growth; 13% negative in 2024, 16% in 2023) with high volatility among carriers ranked 31–50.
  • Small-carrier risks: carriers below ~200 in rank average only ~$36M NWP, many are broker-controlled or undercapitalized, lack technology/reinsurance/internal controls, and must “write the cream,” which creates adverse selection for struggling admitted carriers and raises regulatory oversight concerns.
  • Mutuals vs. stock carriers: stock carriers (especially those with scale) have generally outperformed mutuals in premium and surplus growth and profit margins; several large mutuals have been stagnant or unprofitable for a decade and may continue to decline or be sold.
  • Alternative markets and selection pressure: captives, RRGs, SIGs and surplus lines are selectively writing highly profitable accounts, increasing adverse selection pressure on traditional admitted markets and widening product/rate/surplus differentials.
