Insurers need to update cat models for “supershear” risks to prevent further related losses: MS Amlin - Reinsurance News

Source: www.reinsurancene.ws · Score: 0.80
Why it matters: MS Amlin research highlights underestimation of earthquake risk due to supershear ruptures and its impact on pricing/capital for catastrophe models—relevant to Lloyd's market risk and underwriting.
  • Supershear earthquakes—ruptures that propagate faster than shear waves—produce stronger, longer‑range shaking and unusual torsional forces; MS Amlin attributes ~2/3 of insured earthquake losses in the last decade to supershear events (≈$13.2bn).
  • Material model bias: current catastrophe models can materially understate exposure—MS Amlin finds portfolio losses rise ~5–10% at a 200‑year return period and ~30–60% at a 500‑year return period when supershear effects are included—creating a capital and pricing blindspot, particularly for California (San Andreas) and other long strike‑slip faults.
  • Rising relevance: improved detection shows supershear involvement in ~36% of major strike‑slip earthquakes since 2010, and recent examples (e.g., 2025 Myanmar M7.7) demonstrate much longer ruptures and wider areas of shaking than previously estimated.
  • Practical recommendations: insurers should urgently run stress tests and sensitivity scenarios for supershear rupture and alternative shaking patterns, map/expose portfolios to long strike‑slip faults, and press catastrophe model vendors to incorporate supershear physics into next‑generation hazard modules before finalising pricing and capital decisions.
