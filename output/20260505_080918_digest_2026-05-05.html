Corporate buyers increasingly filling gaps with parametric cover - Business Insurance

Source: www.businessinsurance.com · Score: 0.75
Why it matters: Discusses parametric insurance adoption and coverage trends relevant to Lloyd's market risk transfer.
  • Parametric insurance is gaining adoption among corporate buyers (hospitality, energy, utilities, industrial and public sector) to fill gaps in traditional property programs and expand catastrophe risk financing.
  • Buyers typically use parametrics to fund deductibles, finance emergency response and cover hard‑to‑insure or nonphysical business interruption exposures; policies pay on event intensity (wind speed, quake shaking, hail size) rather than measured loss.
  • Common structures range from ~$10–30 million (sweet spot ~$25–30M) up to $100 million for U.S. hurricane and earthquake covers; minimum premiums start around $50,000 for perils like flood/tornado/hail/quake and about $100,000 for hurricane.
  • Parametric products offer faster, more transparent settlements — Swiss Re reported 16 U.S. event payouts since 2020 with an average payment time of ~12 days and ~80% credited within 15 days — driving greater corporate demand since major 2017 hurricanes.
