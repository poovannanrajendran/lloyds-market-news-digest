Nordic allocators increasingly see cat bonds as fixed income / alternatives complement: Markets Group - Artemis.bm

Source: www.artemis.bm · Score: 0.72
Why it matters: Covers insurance-linked securities and catastrophe bonds as structured investments and diversification for insurers, directly related to Lloyd's focus on catastrophe risk and reinsurance.
  • Nordic institutional allocators are increasingly considering ILS — particularly catastrophe bonds — as a complement to fixed income and alternatives because traditional diversification has weakened and they need return drivers independent of financial-market cycles.
  • Cat bonds are attractive due to a structural, event-driven return driver (natural catastrophes) that is largely uncorrelated with interest rates, credit spreads and equities; instruments are typically fully collateralized with principal at risk only if a defined trigger is met.
  • Allocators stress rigorous modelling, transparent trigger mechanics and accurate pricing of peril exposures (eg US hurricane, earthquake) — viewing loss risk as bounded and event‑specific rather than systemic, which supports holding through post‑event volatility when well understood.
  • Practical implementation: firms treat cat bonds either as a fixed‑income complement or in an alternatives/real‑assets sleeve, but require bespoke governance, due diligence, risk reporting and sizing discipline to oversee this non‑traditional risk and avoid mistimed exits.
