ADB aims to issue $120m-$150m parametric cat bond for Kyrgyz Republic & Tajikistan in H1’26 - Artemis.bm

Source: www.artemis.bm · Score: 0.78
Why it matters: ADB plans first catastrophe bond (DRB) using ILS/parametric trigger, details for Kyrgyz Republic and Tajikistan; aligns with Lloyd's market coverage of cat bonds.
  • ADB to issue its first 3-year parametric catastrophe bond (Disaster Relief Bond) of $120–150m under its GMTN program to cover earthquake and flood risk for Kyrgyz Republic and Tajikistan; ADB will manage structuring, issuance and payments without an SPV, exposing investors to ADB (AAA) credit + specified catastrophe risk rather than sovereign risk.
  • Parametric triggers (including excess‑rainfall for floods) validated by a third‑party loss agent will trigger rapid, pre‑defined payouts routed directly to national governments to provide immediate liquidity for disaster response—typical sovereign cat‑bond speed and transparency.
  • Each country receives a $15m ADF grant to support premiums, leveraging 4–5x coverage to target approximately $50m earthquake cover and $10–25m flood cover per country; CAREC modelling estimates 1‑in‑25‑year events (e.g., ~$485m earthquake damage in Kyrgyz → ~$12.5m DRB payout).
  • Key execution risks include policy reforms, institutional capacity and market acceptance; ADB plans technical assistance and international‑standard structuring to attract institutional investors and mitigate implementation delays.

PERILS estimates Windstorm Goretti insured market loss of €467m - Artemis.bm

Source: www.artemis.bm · Score: 0.75
Why it matters: European windstorm loss estimate and insurance market impact relevant to Lloyd's market news.
  • PERILS’ initial industry-insured loss estimate for Windstorm Goretti (8–9 Jan) is EUR 467 million, based on data from the majority of impacted insurers and covering property and motor hull lines.
  • Meteorology: Goretti featured explosive cyclogenesis and a sting jet, producing record gusts (213 km/h at Gatteville) plus high waves, rain, snow and ice — locally severe but within the range of annual European windstorm losses.
  • Loss geography and concentration: roughly 75% of losses occurred in France; the UK was largely spared aside from Cornwall and the Channel Islands, and Belgium was only marginally impacted; a more northerly track would have driven materially higher losses.
  • Significance and next steps: Goretti is the largest European windstorm loss to date for the 2025/26 season and the first to exceed PERILS’ capture threshold this season; PERILS will publish an updated estimate on 9 April 2026.
