Arthur Re Ltd. – Woody Re 2026-1

Source: www.artemis.bm · Score: 0.86
Why it matters: Catastrophe bond benefiting Lloyd's Syndicate 3123; explicitly tied to Lloyd's market exposure and administration.
  • First cat bond to benefit Lloyd’s Syndicate 3123 (launched 2024 by the Fidelis Partnership with Pelagos), issued via Gallagher Re’s Arthur Re Ltd. platform as Woody Re 2026‑1 Class A (initial tranche $75m) to provide fully‑collateralized multi‑year, multi‑peril retrocessional reinsurance.
  • Three‑year term to end June 2029; covers named storms, earthquakes, severe thunderstorms, winter storms and wildfire on an annual‑aggregate, industry loss index trigger basis (franchise deductibles: $15bn for named storm & earthquake; $10bn for other perils).
  • Geographic scope: all perils cover the 50 US states + DC; named storms and earthquakes also include Puerto Rico and USVI, and earthquake cover additionally includes Canada; severe thunderstorms, wildfires and winter storms limited to US + DC.
  • Financial attachment/exhaustion and risk metrics: initial attachment at $78bn of aggregated industry losses, exhaustion at $148bn; initial attachment probability ~7.13% and initial expected loss ~3.88%.

GIFT City is attracting global reinsurance capital at scale, says PB Fintech’s Singh - Reinsurance News

Source: www.reinsurancene.ws · Score: 0.78
Why it matters: Discusses GIFT City's growth as a global reinsurance hub and regulatory framework attracting international (re)insurers, relevant to Lloyd's market.
  • Unified, internationally aligned IFSCA framework + 100% FDI in GIFT City removes entry barriers (single regulatory regime, allowance to operate under home‑country solvency norms), making market entry straightforward and enabling global reinsurers to use existing capital pools.
  • Compelling tax and capital economics: Section 80LA offers 100% tax exemption for 10 years within a 15‑year window and the ability to leverage home‑regulator solvency rules frees trapped capital, lowering cost of capital and improving deployment efficiency.
  • Rapid, measurable growth: insurance and reinsurance volumes at GIFT City rose from $102m in 2020 to $1.2bn in 2025 (>11x); IIOs increased 24→36 and IIIOs 31→34 (Dec 2025→Mar 2026); Q4 GWP: direct insurance $15.38m (from $9.25m) and reinsurance $186.93m (from $148.13m).
  • Strategic impact for insurers/reinsurers: GIFT City is positioning as an international reinsurance hub (comparable to Singapore/Dubai/London), attracting global reinsurers/brokers who can establish fully owned, tax‑efficient hubs and bring proprietary underwriting, risk models and governance to scale India‑linked risks.

Shipowners Seek Clarity on Hormuz Deal as 600 Vessels Eye Exit

Source: www.insurancejournal.com · Score: 0.72
Why it matters: Discusses shipping risk, insurance considerations and market implications of the Hormuz disruption and potential reopening.
  • Potential reopening of the Strait of Hormuz may occur within days, but shipowners and traders are highly cautious after months of false starts and limited public detail on transit conditions.
  • Operational picture: ~600 vessels remain stuck in the Persian Gulf with hundreds waiting outside; tankers (crude and dirty products) are the bulk, so a surge of movements and corridor congestion is likely if transits resume.
  • Security and liability risks persist—previous incidents include firing and seizures, and mine threats remain plausible—making route safety and the scope of insurance cover (war/strikes, P&I, hull/cargo) critical uncertainties.
  • Practical impediments (hull biofouling, port/crew logistics) and a likely phased return led by more risk-tolerant owners mean underwriters should reassess premiums, exclusions, voyage contingency planning, and monitor AIS/transponder blackout gaps closely.
