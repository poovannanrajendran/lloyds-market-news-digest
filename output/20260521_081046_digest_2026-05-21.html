Briefing: The price of expertise in professional services

Source: www.insurancetimes.co.uk · Score: 0.65
Why it matters: Discusses insurance pricing, broker models, and AI-related risk—core issues for the Lloyd's market.
  • The Sullivan & Cromwell AI “hallucination” episode is a cautionary tale: beyond AI-specific risk, it exposes a fundamental problem — current market practice struggles to price and allocate accountability for professional expertise.
  • Legal firms price by seniority and carry internal partner-level accountability; by contrast, insurance brokers commonly use blended commissions that dilute transparency of who delivered the expertise and spread reputational/PI risk across the firm.
  • Practical levers to align price with accountability include transparent tiering of broker/advisory expertise, charging for quotes or capacity access, minimum premium commitments, and creating tradable capacity instruments (e.g., annual capacity options).
  • Implication for insurers and brokers: as AI reshapes risk and efficiency, the industry should reassess how it values and charges for expertise and capacity — structural changes could improve incentives, risk allocation, and perceived value across buyer, broker and carrier.
