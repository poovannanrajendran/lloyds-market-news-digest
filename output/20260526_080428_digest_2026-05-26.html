Plenty of capital in the Australian reinsurance market, aggregate appetite returns: Grant Hollyman - Reinsurance News

Source: www.reinsurancene.ws · Score: 0.80
Why it matters: Mentions Lloyd's rating upgrade (A+ to AA-), tying Australian reinsurance market discussion to Lloyd's capital/credit quality.
  • Reinsurers are increasingly willing to provide aggregate covers (second, third, fourth/fifth event protections), with renewed appetite after the hard market—still not easy to buy but more prevalent and often priced on a burning‑cost basis.
  • There is abundant and improving reinsurance capacity and capital in Australia/New Zealand, creating more favourable buying conditions and increased supply across property and casualty markets.

Air France, Airbus found guilty in AF447 crash - Business Insurance

Source: www.businessinsurance.com · Score: 0.75
Why it matters: Aviation liability ruling could affect underwriting and claims handling in Lloyd's market.
  • Paris Appeals Court found Air France and Airbus guilty of corporate manslaughter over the 2009 AF447 crash, attributing responsibility to systemic operational failings tied to known technical faults and insufficient pilot preparedness rather than solely individual pilot error.
  • Ruling broadens corporate liability exposure for airlines and manufacturers—raising the prospect of increased frequency and severity of insured aviation liability claims, criminal-liability exposures, and regulatory penalties.
  • Market impacts likely include upward pressure on premiums, tighter underwriting, higher retentions or exclusions for product/operational liability, and potential changes in D&O, hull & liability and manufacturers’ cover placement and pricing.
  • Practical implications for insurers and brokers: review policy wordings (gross negligence, criminal acts, product defect exclusions), reassess underwriting criteria (training, maintenance, systems design), prepare for more complex subrogation/recovery efforts, and monitor for cross‑jurisdictional precedent and reinsurance contagion.

NCDEX launches India’s first exchange-traded parametric weather derivative RAINMUMBAI - Artemis.bm

Source: www.artemis.bm · Score: 0.72
Why it matters: India's first SEBI-approved weather derivative; relevant to insurance/reinsurance risk hedging.
  • NCDEX has launched RAINMUMBAI — India’s first SEBI‑approved exchange‑traded parametric weather derivative — providing a regulated, tradable hedging instrument for Mumbai rainfall risk.
  • The contract is based on a Cumulative Deviation Rainfall (CDR) index vs a 1991–2020 long‑period average, built using IMD surface station data and IIT Bombay modelling to ensure objective, transparent parametric settlement.
  • Target users include agriculture, construction, utilities, energy, retail, infrastructure, banking and logistics; the product offers faster, basis‑driven payouts and a complement to indemnity insurance and government relief.
  • Implications for insurers/reinsurers: useful for portfolio hedging, product bundling and ILS development, but requires attention to basis risk, trigger design, spatial coverage (Mumbai region) and alignment with insured loss profiles.

Source: www.businessinsurance.com · Score: 0.70
