Company vehicle didn’t make fatal commute compensable: Texas appeals - Business Insurance

Source: www.businessinsurance.com · Score: 0.75
Why it matters: Impact on workers' compensation coverage for employer-provided vehicle commuting and related risk.
  • Texas 11th Court of Appeals affirmed summary judgment for Old Republic: the insurer need not pay workers’ compensation death benefits after an Energy Transfer technician was killed driving home in a company vehicle.
  • Court found the technician was commuting home unpaid after completing his shift and was not responding to a callout; therefore he was not acting in the course and scope of employment.
  • The court emphasized that mere use of an employer-owned vehicle does not establish that travel originated in the employer’s business, so a provided vehicle alone does not make a commute compensable.
  • Practical takeaways for insurers and employers: carefully document employee status at time of incident (paid time, callouts, assignments), maintain clear company-vehicle and on-call policies, and ensure claims investigations and policy language address commute versus work-related travel exposure.

Topgolf wins reversal of additional temporary disability benefits - Business Insurance

Source: www.businessinsurance.com · Score: 0.72
Why it matters: US workers' compensation ruling affecting disability benefits and treatment obligations; relevant to claims and coverage considerations.
  • Tennessee Workers’ Compensation Appeals Board (Jayne‑Anne Patrick v. Topgolf, decision June 29, 2026) reversed a trial‑court award of additional temporary disability (TTD) after a second physician recommended revision knee arthroscopy; claimant had originally reached MMI in June 2025 with a 1% impairment and no restrictions, and Topgolf had ended TTD.
  • The board held that a later recommendation for further treatment, by itself, does not undo a prior MMI finding. To obtain additional TTD after MMI, the claimant must produce medical evidence showing the original MMI determination was premature or should be withdrawn.
  • The appeals board affirmed the order requiring Topgolf to provide additional medical treatment that the employer had agreed to, highlighting the legal distinction between entitlement to medical care and entitlement to indemnity benefits.
  • Practical implications for carriers/TPAs: document and support MMI determinations thoroughly; require objective medical proof before reopening indemnity payments; continue to authorize agreed medical care where appropriate while contesting additional TTD exposure; and monitor jurisdictional standards for retracting MMI.
