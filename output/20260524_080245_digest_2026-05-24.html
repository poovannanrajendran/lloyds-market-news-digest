Source: www.artemis.bm · Score: 0.78
Why it matters: Discusses catastrophe bond issuance and ILS market activity, impacting Lloyd's reinsurance environment.
  • H1 2026 catastrophe bond issuance is now projected at $16.3bn, with $14.4bn already completed/settled (over $14.1bn as 144A bonds and ~$13.72bn of property cat bonds).
  • Deal pipeline and volume: 60 transactions settled so far plus ~12 offerings in the pipeline (≥72 deals expected in H1), leaving scope to surpass H1 2025’s $17.56bn if more deals upsize or new ones settle before June 30.

Mid-year renewals seen down 15-20%+, cat bonds more of a competitive threat: Dutt, Aeolus - Artemis.bm

Source: www.artemis.bm · Score: 0.72
Why it matters: Covers mid-year reinsurance pricing dynamics, ILS/catastrophe bonds, and market competition relevant to Lloyd's market.
  • Mid-year 2026 property catastrophe reinsurance pricing is down ~15–20% year-on-year, skewing toward the worst end of that range; US nationwide accounts and Florida are the most competitive, with retrocession pricing also easing.
  • Catastrophe bonds are the fastest-growing and most competitive segment of ILS, increasingly threatening traditional reinsurance by offering longer-duration cover and lower protection rates.
  • The sector is over-capitalised; incremental reinsurance demand of roughly $10–15bn is insufficient to counter pricing pressure, and cedants are largely retaining reinsurance savings rather than expanding purchase programs.
  • Terms and conditions are broadly holding (attachment points largely stable), but meaningful market hardening would likely require ~20% removal of capital (~$135bn of losses); absent a major loss, prices could weaken further into the January 2027 renewals.

Mayflower Re Ltd. (Series 2026-1)

Source: www.artemis.bm · Score: 0.70
Why it matters: Involves catastrophe bonds and reinsurance market activity (MPIUA/Hannover Re) relevant to Lloyd's market.
  • Sponsor and structure: Massachusetts FAIR Plan (MPIUA) is returning to the cat bond market using Bermuda vehicle Mayflower Re Ltd. for its seventh issuance, targeting $150m aggregate across two Class A tranches; proceeds will collateralize a retrocessional reinsurance agreement with Hannover Re acting as fronting risk transformer.
  • Term and coverage: Multi‑year indemnity-based and annual aggregate reinsurance over a three‑year term with three annual risk periods to June 2029, covering Massachusetts named storms, severe thunderstorms and winter storms; events must exceed a $10m ultimate net loss deductible to aggregate.
  • Series 2026‑1 Class A: $75m tranche attaching at $850m and exhausting at $1.3bn of MPIUA pool losses; initial attachment probability 2.196%, base expected loss 1.788%, marketed spreads ~4.0%–4.5%.
  • Series 2026‑1 Class B: $75m tranche attaching at $550m and exhausting at $850m of MPIUA pool losses; initial attachment probability 3.079%, base expected loss 2.598%, marketed spreads ~4.75%–5.25%.

Allstate starts 2026 annual aggregate risk period with $870m of April catastrophe losses - Artemis.bm

Source: www.artemis.bm · Score: 0.70
Why it matters: Covers catastrophe bonds and aggregate reinsurance exposure, relevant to Lloyd's market risk discussions, though not Lloyd's-specific.
  • Allstate’s annual aggregate risk period began April 1; the company reported $870m pre-tax ($687m after-tax) catastrophe losses in April 2026 from 10 wind/hail events, with roughly 70% of losses tied to two events.
  • Allstate currently has only one annual aggregate catastrophe bond tranche (Sanders Re III Class B, $150m). That tranche attaches at $4.78bn and runs to $5.28bn (US-wide except Florida) and carries a $50m per‑event deductible — meaning only events ≥$50m and losses reaching that high attachment can erode its retention, so only a portion of April’s $870m is likely to count toward the cat‑bond layer.
  • At 2026 renewal Allstate bought a $1bn aggregate excess‑of‑loss reinsurance layer attaching at $8.5bn with a $1m per‑event deductible. Given the low event deductible, the majority of April losses are expected to qualify to erode the retention beneath this reinsurance, so a material share of that retention could be consumed.
  • April’s $870m pre‑tax result is near recent peak April experience (2023 $872m; 2025 $594m; 2024 $494m). The timing and concentration of losses early in the aggregate period increases the risk of meaningful reinsurance/retention erosion — monitor further loss development and layer attachment/exhaustion.
