Rochdale sees elevated industry-loss warranty (ILW) demand as mutual fund returns 11.27% - Artemis.bm

Source: www.artemis.bm · Score: 0.72
Why it matters: Discusses ILW/catastrophe bonds and investor demand affecting insurance markets, topics closely followed in Lloyd's market news.
  • City National Rochdale Select Strategies Fund returned a net 11.27% for the year to Jan 31, 2026 (eighth consecutive positive year) with total net assets of $234.51m, slightly up from $228m a year earlier.
  • The fund is a 1940 Act mutual ILS strategy that gains exposure primarily to industry-loss warranties (ILWs) and largely index-trigger catastrophe bonds by allocating capital to NB RE Ltd. cells managed by Neuberger Berman’s ILS team.
  • Market dynamics: demand for 2026 ILW protection has been significantly elevated ahead of the January renewals and cat bond primary issuance hit record levels for the third consecutive year, expanding and diversifying the investable universe.
  • Positioning and outlook: the portfolio was defensively structured for an active Atlantic hurricane season with reduced non-peak exposure; managers highlight rising frequency perils (SCS and wildfires drove >80% of insured losses) and expect attractive, capital-efficient ILW and cat bond opportunities to support risk-adjusted returns and limit recent price softening.
