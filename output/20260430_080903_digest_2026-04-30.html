PRA consults on funded reinsurance capital reform for UK insurers - Reinsurance News

Source: www.reinsurancene.ws · Score: 0.82
Why it matters: UK PRA consultation on funded reinsurance capital treatment; directly affects UK insurance market and Lloyd's investment landscape.
  • PRA consultation would re‑calibrate capital for funded reinsurance to better reflect reinsurer credit and collateral risk — current practice typically holds ~2–4% of annuity liabilities vs ~11–15% for comparable investments; PRA expects capital for new funded reinsurance to rise to around 10%.
  • Practical impact: higher capital charges will increase the cost of funded reinsurance and reduce incentives to substitute it for direct investment or other capital solutions, with potential pricing, structuring and hedging implications for bulk purchase annuity (BPA) transactions. UK insurers currently have ~£40bn exposure and usage is growing.
  • Implementation and actions: changes apply only to new arrangements from 1 October (not retrospective). Firms should review capital models, counterparty credit assessments, collateral eligibility and legal/operational documentation to quantify the effect on solvency and deal economics.
  • Policy and context: the proposals are designed to strengthen policyholder protection (FSCS remains the backstop) and sit within the wider Solvency UK reforms alongside measures such as the Matching Adjustment Investment Accelerator and Strong and Simple reforms; expect supervisory scrutiny and stress‑test linkage.

UK Gov to legislate for risk transformation and ILS flexibility to promote innovation and growth - Artemis.bm

Source: www.artemis.bm · Score: 0.80
Why it matters: UK government response outlines reforms to risk transformation and ILS/catastrophe bond regime, affecting Lloyd's market participants and transactions in the UK.
  • UK government will press primary and secondary legislation to liberalise the Risk Transformation Regulations to boost ILS/cat bond activity—changes target ISPVs and PCCs to simplify authorisation and expand permissible transaction structures.
  • Funding and capital treatment will become more flexible: primary legislation will clarify that the PRA can permit non‑fully paid‑in financing, set which assets or alternative financing mechanisms are acceptable, and vary valuation/requirements by type of transferred business, while retaining the core requirement that assets cover maximum liabilities.
  • PCC and authorisation flexibility: reforms will enable cells to assume multiple risks from multiple cedants and amend regulation 7 so the PRA is not required to limit a transformer vehicle’s permissions at authorisation, allowing supervisory discretion to align permissions with business evolution.
  • Non‑insurer cedants (e.g., corporates or sovereigns) are not being opened up by statute yet—government will continue work with the PRA and FCA to assess safeguards and overlaps with other regulated activities before any legislative change.

AIG unit must cover portion of $26 million SEC settlement - Business Insurance

Source: www.businessinsurance.com · Score: 0.72
Why it matters: D&O coverage dispute involving SEC disgorgement; implications for D&O insurance practices relevant to Lloyd's market.
  • Delaware Superior Court ordered Illinois National Insurance Co. (an AIG unit) to indemnify Clear Channel for $16.4 million in SEC-ordered disgorgement plus $3.8 million in prejudgment interest arising from a 2023 SEC FCPA enforcement action tied to a Chinese subsidiary’s alleged bribery and inadequate controls.
  • AIG argued disgorgement was an uninsured civil penalty and that coverage would violate Delaware public policy; the court rejected both, finding the policy clearly distinguished civil monetary penalties (excluded) from disgorgement (covered), and that public‑policy arguments require a strong showing to override contract terms.
  • The award is limited to the D&O policy limit (the disgorgement and interest exhaust that limit); a separate dispute over defense costs was dismissed as moot, and Clear Channel’s bad‑faith claim failed because AIG’s coverage positions were not deemed clearly unreasonable.
  • Implications for insurance professionals: review D&O policy wording on “civil penalties” versus “disgorgement,” anticipate potential coverage for SEC disgorgement absent explicit exclusion, and note Delaware courts are generally reluctant to void coverage on public‑policy grounds or find bad faith without a strong factual basis.
