Source: www.artemis.bm · Score: 0.75
Why it matters: Covers sidecar market dynamics, valuations, and capital-market trends relevant to Lloyd's market activities.
  • Sidecar market is expanding beyond short‑tailed property cat into casualty and multi‑line, introducing long‑tail dynamics tied to social and economic inflation that increase macro sensitivity and reserve uncertainty.
  • Valuation is critical: reserve development (timing and ultimate size) drives value in casualty sidecars, so Kroll emphasizes sensitivity analysis, partnering with actuaries, and pricing to where a transaction would clear as of the valuation date rather than relying blindly on models.

  • Regulators now have broader powers to detain shipments suspected of containing goods made with forced labor, increasing the likelihood of cargo seizures, delays and disrupted trade flows.

Banned breeds: Has the insurance market left dog attack victims exposed?

Source: www.insurancetimes.co.uk · Score: 0.71
Why it matters: UK changes remove third-party liability requirement for exempted banned dogs; insurer withdrawal and market implications for the insurance sector.
  • Mandatory third‑party liability cover for government‑registered exempted banned dogs was removed on 1 July after the only provider of suitable insurance withdrew, so owners are no longer legally required to buy cover.
  • Owners remain legally liable for damage caused by their dogs, but in practice victims face difficulty recovering compensation from uninsured owners, creating a protection gap for serious attacks.
  • Insurers’ withdrawal reflects underwriting realities: high severity and historical claim frequency for breeds like XL Bullies, small exposed population, behavioural and environmental complexity, and volatility that makes standalone liability cover commercially unviable.
  • Defra characterises the change as market‑driven rather than a deliberate policy shift; ministers removed the requirement because compliance became impossible, highlighting a market failure forcing regulatory adjustment.
