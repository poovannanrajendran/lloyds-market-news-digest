  • PGGM’s ILS/reinsurance portfolio delivered a 12.4% return in USD for full‑year 2025, and PGGM remains the largest pension/sovereign investor listed in the ILS/reinsurance directory.

Catastrophe bonds offer diversification to investors when they need it most: Poreda, Sage Advisory - Artemis.bm

Source: www.artemis.bm · Score: 0.72
Why it matters: Covers catastrophe bonds/ILS and reinsurance pricing trends relevant to the Lloyd's market.
  • Catastrophe bonds offer genuine diversification versus traditional assets: low correlation and structural independence, generating positive returns in ~79% of equity down months (average +0.25%) while the S&P 500 averaged −4.2% in those same months (10‑year period to 5 May 2026).
  • Resilience across cycles—cat bond performance often recovers/outsizes after loss years: Swiss Re Global Cat Bond Index +19.7% (2023) and +17.3% (2024); post‑2005 returns of +12.0% (2006) and +15.4% (2007); spreads widened materially after events (e.g., Hurricane Ian: ~7% to >11%).
  • Industry cost pressures (social inflation, rising litigation severity, climate risk) are increasing reinsurance/cat bond pricing, which can raise the forward expected returns for cat bond investors as insurers pass on higher loss costs.
  • Key risks to manage: imperfect catastrophe models and potential model drift from climate change; single‑event losses can fully write down specific tranches; liquidity can deteriorate and capital may be temporarily trapped after major events.
