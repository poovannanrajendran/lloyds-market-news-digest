Why it matters: ICMR's launch of Lloyd's market investor NAV delta monitoring and related reporting tools (RISX linkage).
  • ICMR has launched a market‑wide monthly NAV‑delta monitoring capability for Lloyd’s to serve as an additional benchmark alongside its portfolio‑specific independent NAV reporting.

ICMR launches NAV Index to enhance transparency of investing at Lloyd's for capital markets - Artemis.bm

Source: www.artemis.bm · Score: 0.85
Why it matters: Introduces a new monthly NAV index for the Lloyd’s market to improve investor transparency and capital deployment decisions.
  • ICMR has launched a monthly NAV Index as a new benchmark tracking the monthly NAV delta for the Lloyd’s insurance and reinsurance market to improve transparency and support capital-deployment decisions for Lloyd’s investors.
  • The index uses a proprietary methodology that interpolates 20 years of published annual data into monthly results, adjusts for known major claims and investment events in real time, and is rigorously backtested against reported Lloyd’s pro forma results.
  • ICMR integrates its ICMR.Insight AI relative-performance model to produce forward-looking probabilistic outputs, supporting both historical backtesting and forecast-reasonableness testing.
  • Practical benefits for insurers and investors include timelier portfolio performance review, improved mid-year valuations and underwriting year planning, alignment with capital-market investor expectations, and complementary insight alongside ICMR’s RISX equity index and growing investor structures (e.g., London Bridge).

Vehicle value swings of up to £7,000 could leave policyholders facing insurance gap

Source: www.insurancetimes.co.uk · Score: 0.72
Why it matters: Discusses vehicle valuations impacting insurance underwriting and claims, relevant to Lloyd's market.
  • Used-car resale values are highly volatile (e.g. Audi Q7 up ~£7k, Range Rover Evoque down ~£5k in 12 months), meaning vehicle market values can swing materially in months and materially affect claim outcomes and exposures.
  • Policyholder impacts: falling market values increase risk of underinsurance on total-loss claims; agreed-value policies can result in underpayment to the customer when values rise before payout.
  • Insurer impacts: accurate, retail-backed, point-in-time valuations are critical for underwriting discipline, pricing, reserving and portfolio risk management—trade-in or outdated inception values are inadequate.
  • Recommended actions: adopt live market valuation data at renewal (or on request), proactively communicate valuation changes to customers in line with FCA Consumer Duty, and revalue portfolios regularly to reduce mispricing and improve customer outcomes.
