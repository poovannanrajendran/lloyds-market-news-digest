CHAPS

Source: www.bankofengland.co.uk · Score: 0.65
Why it matters: Mentions CHAPS system and participants (including Lloyds Bank), a UK settlement infrastructure topic relevant to Lloyd's market operations.
  • CHAPS is the UK’s real‑time gross settlement (RTGS) system operating 06:00–18:00 Mon–Fri (excl. bank holidays); payments submitted by 17:40 for customer credit are settled irrevocably and same day between direct participants.
  • Used for high‑value, time‑sensitive flows relevant to insurers: interbank money‑market/FX settlement, corporate supplier/tax payments, property completions and high‑value customer transactions (e.g., large claims, deposits).
  • Settlement finality between direct participants removes settlement risk but increases intraday liquidity requirements and makes operational resilience and SWIFT connectivity critical — important considerations for treasury, claims payment timing and counterparty exposure.
  • Access and compliance: direct participation requires a Bank of England RTGS account, legal and technical criteria (SWIFT BICs, interfaces, BERTI, EISCD), ongoing self‑attestation and reporting, and adherence to CHAPS reimbursement rules — indirect participants may be in‑scope for reporting and reimbursement obligations.
