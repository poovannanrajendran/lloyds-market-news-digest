Swiss Re launches Alternative Capital Solutions team led by Wiget, for ILS and retro hedging - Artemis.bm

Source: www.artemis.bm · Score: 0.78
Why it matters: Swiss Re's launch of an Alternative Capital Solutions unit focusing on ILS and retrocession hedging is a relevant market development for the Lloyd's re/insurance ecosystem.
  • Swiss Re will launch an Alternative Capital Solutions unit on July 1 to centralise hedging and execution across insurance‑linked securities (ILS) and retrocessional trades that benefit Swiss Re’s own balance sheet.
  • Mirjam Wiget will lead the new team; she is a long‑standing Swiss Re executive (joined 2011) with senior roles in treasury, group performance, strategy and FP&A, giving her oversight experience in financial performance, risk and capital management.
  • The new unit will report into Martin Zingg (recently appointed head of Alternative Capital Partners); reporting lines for managers of third‑party ILS funds remain unchanged.
  • Nick Meuli, formerly Head of Retro Hedging and Execution, is understood to be departing, and Swiss Re has also re‑entered the catastrophe bond market with a new retro cat bond.

  • Class E ($50m, seasonal to Dec 2026, per-occurrence named storm, Northeast US) attaches at $30bn–$35bn; attach prob 2.35%, base expected loss 2.19%; structured as discount notes, price guidance 95.25%–95.75% of par.

Arch Capital secures 50% upsized $150m Ramble Re 2026-1 retro cat bond at low-end pricing - Artemis.bm

Source: www.artemis.bm · Score: 0.72
Why it matters: Catastrophe bond issuance and retrocession deal impacting Lloyd's reinsurance market.
  • Arch Capital upsized its Ramble Re Ltd. Series 2026-1 cat bond sponsorship from $100m to $150m and secured the full amount as a single, fully‑collateralized Class A tranche.
  • The $150m notes were priced at a 5.0% initial risk interest spread (bottom of the revised 5.0%–5.5% guidance) with an initial expected loss of 3.17%, reflecting strong investor demand.
  • Three‑year retrocession cover: per‑occurrence and weighted industry loss index triggers, protecting against significant US Northeast named storms and US/Canada earthquake losses.
  • Twice‑reduced price guidance and successful upsizing illustrate continued sponsor‑favourable conditions in the cat bond/ILS market and robust investor appetite for peak peril retrocession.

Achmea secures one-third upsized €100m Windmill III Re 2026-1 catastrophe bond - Artemis.bm

Source: www.artemis.bm · Score: 0.72
Why it matters: Details of a European windstorm catastrophe bond (Windmill III Re 2026-1) upsizing, pricing and in-force protections—topic of interest to Lloyd's market/ILS coverage.
  • Achmea upsized its Windmill III Re DAC Series 2026-1 cat bond to €100m of collateralized European windstorm reinsurance, pricing below initial guidance at a 3.75% risk interest spread.
  • The notes provide a four‑year, indemnity‑triggered, per‑occurrence cover for European windstorm and specified severe convective perils (including hail and tornadoes); initial expected loss is 2.58%.
  • This is Achmea’s fifth Windmill cat bond and the first time it will have two Windmill issuances in force simultaneously (combined €200m protection, with the 2024‑1 cover running until end‑June 2028), reflecting a deliberate staggering of maturities.
  • Compared with Windmill III Re 2024‑1 (which priced at 5.25% with a 2.19% expected loss), the lower 2026‑1 spread signals market softening and strong investor appetite for European peril diversification, contributing to spread compression.
