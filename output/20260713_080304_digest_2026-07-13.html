UK to Directly Regulate Cloud Service Providers to Protect Stability of Financial Sector

Source: www.insurancejournal.com · Score: 0.75
Why it matters: UK designates cloud providers as critical third parties to the financial sector, impacting insurers and market infrastructure governed by BoE/PRA/FCA.
  • UK has designated Microsoft Ireland, Google Cloud EMEA, Amazon Web Services EMEA and Oracle UK as “critical third-party” cloud suppliers to the financial sector (effective July 13) and placed them under joint supervision by the Bank of England, PRA and FCA.
  • Designated providers must undergo resilience testing, perform regular self-assessments and report major incidents — increasing regulatory scrutiny of cloud operational resilience and incident transparency.
  • For insurers this heightens third‑party risk expectations: review cloud contracts, SLAs, contingency/business continuity plans, data residency and exit/migration arrangements to evidence resilience and compliance.
  • Cross‑border implications: UK’s focused 4‑provider approach differs from the EU’s broader 19‑firm framework, so insurers operating internationally must align governance and reporting to multiple regimes.
