Rule 144A cat bonds reach 80% adoption as Southeast inverts historical market norms: Gallagher Re’s Schwebach - Artemis.bm

Source: www.artemis.bm · Score: 0.72
Why it matters: Covers reinsurance market dynamics, ILS/cat bonds, and renewal pricing affecting capacity and pricing in regions Lloyd's covers.
  • Rule 144A catastrophe bond market has become integral to Southeast property reinsurance programs—Gallagher Re estimates 80%+ of clients now utilise Rule 144A cat bonds as part of traditional placements versus ~20% five years ago.
  • Early‑2026 renewals saw attractive cat‑bond pricing and upsized issuances; by mid‑year pricing reached parity with traditional reinsurance, allowing cedants to compare ILS vs traditional structures (reinstatements vs single‑shot, LAE treatment, multi‑year vs single‑year) and optimise program mixes.
  • Non‑life ILS assets reached $135bn at end‑2025 (+19% YoY); while this influx of alternative capital supports market softening, the pricing easing was broad‑based across layers and not solely driven by ILS—traditional reinsurers are also accessing ILS capacity.
  • Florida and the Southeast saw sizable rate reductions this renewal cycle, driven particularly by demonstrably lower loss costs in Florida after legislative reform, alongside the increased capacity provided by both ILS and traditional markets.

CCRIF grows parametric risk pool 9% to $1.57bn of coverage limits: CEO Anthony - Artemis.bm

Source: www.artemis.bm · Score: 0.72
Why it matters: Discusses catastrophe risk financing, parametric insurance, and ILS developments relevant to the Lloyd's market.
  • CCRIF increased insured coverage 9% to US$1.57 billion (from US$1.44B), with Caribbean portfolio up 17% and Central America up 18%; new members include Jamaica Public Service Company and Nevis Electricity Company, and Jamaica purchased CCRIF’s coast product for fisheries.
  • Operational performance and proof of concept: since 2007 CCRIF has made 82 payouts totaling US$483 million, all delivered within 14 days; the Jamaica response to Hurricane Melissa (CCRIF US$91.9M payout plus US$150M from a catastrophe bond and US$300M contingent credit) illustrates how CCRIF provides rapid liquidity as one layer in a broader financing stack.
  • Climate and hazard context: CCRIF warns a strong El Niño raises drought, heat and food/water insecurity risks; climatologists project fewer named hurricanes but record-warm waters increase the likelihood of sudden extreme rainfall and flash floods — insurers must not equate lower storm counts with lower exposure.
  • Product and strategy expansion: CCRIF is scaling sectoral and micro coverage (Livelihood Protection Policy for farmers/fishers), developing multi-peril agricultural (including drought), housing and anticipatory-action products, and pursuing a 2025–2030 plan to use AI for modeling, expand public–private partnerships, scale to low‑income households, and explore insurance‑linked loans to close the protection gap.

Pension funds in Chile gain appreciation for catastrophe bonds and are allocating: Report - Artemis.bm

Source: www.artemis.bm · Score: 0.70
Why it matters: Discusses catastrophe bonds/ILS market activity and investor allocations in Chile, which can impact Lloyd's market exposure and risk transfer strategies.
  • Chilean pension funds have made their first reported allocations to catastrophe bonds—about US$79 million by end-April—marking a milestone for ILS uptake in the country.
  • Recent issuances that covered Chile (e.g., IBRD CAR 116 in 2018, IBRD–Chile 2023 and Talanx’s Maschpark Re Ltd. Series 2024‑1) plus active outreach by consultants, platforms and specialist ILS managers have raised institutional awareness across Latin America.
  • Pension investor interest is driven by ILS’s structural decorrelation from traditional markets, attractive return profile relative to many fixed‑income instruments, portfolio diversification benefits and demonstrable social impact via disaster risk transfer.
  • Market implication: multi‑year strong performance has spurred capital flows and market expansion, but institutional allocations can lag awareness—so further pension and institutional inflows into ILS/ cat bonds are likely as familiarity and due diligence progress.
