Source: www.reinsurancene.ws · Score: 0.87

If an Endorsement Is Missing from a Policy's Schedule of Forms, Is It Enforceable?

Source: www.insurancejournal.com · Score: 0.66
Why it matters: Discusses endorsements hiding in policy, coverage gaps, and case law affecting policy enforceability—topics pertinent to insurance markets including Lloyd's underwriters and brokers.
  • Hidden endorsements can quietly eliminate coverage—example: a contractor’s CGL policy contained a buried “Contractors Exclusion – Colorado” not listed on the schedule, effectively removing coverage where the insured operated.
  • Agents commonly rely on the schedule of forms; that’s insufficient—inspect the entire policy (RTFP) and renewal packets because carriers may add exclusionary endorsements without clear listing or obvious notice.
  • Courts have weighed in on ‘‘incorporation by reference’’—cases (e.g., National Union, Allstate v. Dean, Lawrence A. Smith) indicate an endorsement that isn’t referenced in the primary policy/declarations may not be enforceable, and state statutes/regulations can further affect outcomes.
  • Key practitioner actions: quality‑control renewals and full policy text (not just form schedules), require that endorsements be listed in declarations or expressly incorporated, verify carrier notice for added exclusions, and check applicable state notice and endorsement rules.
