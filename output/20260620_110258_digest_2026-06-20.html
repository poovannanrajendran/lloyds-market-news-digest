In Focus: How can MGAs sustain growth over the next five years?

Source: www.insurancetimes.co.uk · Score: 0.85
Why it matters: Mentions Lloyd's delegated underwriting and MGA dynamics—central to Lloyd's market news.
  • MGAs are now a core channel in insurance distribution—offering specialist underwriting, speed, close broker relationships and capital-efficient delegated authority (eg. ~45% of Lloyd’s delegated premium)—and continue to attract strong investor appetite and rapid premium growth.
  • Significant near-term opportunities include expanded capacity access in a soft market, private‑equity and US investor inflows, and rising demand for new covers driven by climate, cyber, geopolitical and technology risks; tech and AI enable faster, more granular underwriting and niche productisation.
  • Key risks are the consequences of unchecked rapid growth: boom‑and‑bust cycles, weakening underwriting discipline, margin erosion and loss of carrier support—making profitability, product governance and clear value to capacity providers essential.
  • To thrive over the next five years MGAs should prioritise investment in underwriting talent, data analytics and claims insight, strengthen product governance and distribution partnerships, and balance speed with demonstrable quality and capital/underwriting discipline.

Triple-I reports significant increase in US lightning-related insurance losses during 2025 - Reinsurance News

Source: www.reinsurancene.ws · Score: 0.78
Why it matters: Reports on rising lightning-related homeowners insurance losses in the US, with claim counts, average cost, and regional risk—relevant for insurance, re/insurance markets.
  • 2025 summary: insurers paid an estimated $1.65 billion in lightning-related homeowners claims (up 59% vs. 2024); claim count rose to 61,986 (+11.6%) and the average claim climbed 42.8% to $26,616 (a 146.9% increase since 2017).
  • Geography and severity: Florida, California and Texas had the highest claim volumes; Texas reported the highest average claim ($60,382) and the largest insured losses (~$253M); more than half of claims originated in the top 10 states and total lightning losses were the highest since 2020.
  • Drivers and measurement issues: rising construction/repair costs, inflation, growing prevalence of high‑value electronics/smart‑home tech, power surge damage and litigation pressures are increasing claim severity; some lightning losses may be undercounted when recorded as fire claims and lightning can also trigger wildfires.
  • Implications for insurers: review underwriting and pricing for lightning/surge exposure, ensure policy wordings clarify surge and wildfire coverages, validate CAT and reserve models, promote mitigation (surge/lighting protection) to customers, and prepare claims operations for higher severity and complex electronic/structural losses.
