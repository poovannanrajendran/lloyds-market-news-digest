  • Traditional political violence/terrorism models can materially understate probable maximum loss for data centres because blast effects that breach the structure produce a step-change in damage and loss versus external-only damage, so an engineering-based PML approach is needed.
  • Data centres’ concentrated value, reliance on sensitive internal equipment (servers, cooling, power, networking) and need for continuous operation mean even modest shock, vibration or pressure can cause functional failure and cascading losses.

  • Underwriting, pricing and portfolio management should move beyond templated hazard/geography metrics to assess structural configuration (roof/facade, penetration vulnerability), scenario-specific breach risks and the potential for internal damage propagation, since superficially similar assets can have very different risk profiles.
