Gallagher Re calls for improved AI model assessment methods to support insurance risk pricing - Reinsurance News

Source: www.reinsurancene.ws · Score: 0.85
Why it matters: Insurer-focused AI risk evaluation and pricing implications, including benchmarking limitations and concentration risk, are directly relevant to Lloyd's market news.
  • Current benchmark-based AI evaluations are ill-suited for underwriting: they measure controlled performance but not real-world failure modes, failure frequency, or the potential for correlated failures across an insurer’s portfolio — all critical for accurate pricing.
  • Benchmark contamination and optimisation against standard tests can artificially inflate scores, erode meaningful differentiation between models, and increase systemic concentration risk when many organisations rely on the same foundation models.
  • Restricted-distribution frontier models (e.g., Anthropic’s Mythos) limit independent assessment; without broader access insurers may have to load premiums for uncertainty rather than priced risk, raising costs and slowing market development.
  • Gallagher Re recommends adopting more realistic, adversarial and continuous evaluation (hallucination rates, decision stability, correlated-failure testing) and suggests the re/insurance industry can drive better AI standards through underwriting requirements, pricing structures and coverage design to reward transparency and resilience.
