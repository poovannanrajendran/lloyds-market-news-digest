Truck maker wins partial appeal in D&O coverage dispute - Business Insurance

Source: www.businessinsurance.com · Score: 0.80
Why it matters: Article concerns Lloyd's of London insurers' duty to defend/advance costs in a D&O dispute (Nu Ride), a Lloyd's market issue.
  • New York Appellate Division (First Dept.) held Nu Ride (formerly Lordstown Motors) is entitled to advancement of defense costs under its Oct. 23, 2020–Oct. 23, 2021 claims‑made D&O policy because insurers failed to prove every cause of action depended on excluded pre‑policy conduct.
  • Court applied the retroactive‑date/related‑conduct exclusion narrowly: some allegations may be pre‑policy, but coverage cannot be denied where at least some claims are viable independent of excluded acts.
  • A subpoena issued to an executive during the policy period triggered the insurers’ advancement obligation because it was not limited to pre‑policy conduct; however, subpoenas to the company for SEC documents were properly excluded as not qualifying as covered securities claims.
  • Practical implications for insurers and brokers: review each cause of action separately when applying retroactive‑date exclusions; recognize that in‑period subpoenas or investigations can trigger advancement; and ensure clear drafting of exclusions and claim pleadings to limit coverage disputes.

Gallagher Re warns AI model evaluation is limiting insurer confidence

Source: www.globalreinsurance.com · Score: 0.74
Why it matters: Discusses AI risk evaluation, underwriting implications, and market practices relevant to Lloyd's insurers and the broader re/insurance market.
  • Current benchmark-based evaluations measure capability in controlled tasks but do not reveal how models fail in real-world, ambiguous or adversarial conditions—information insurers need to assess frequency and correlation of failures for underwriting.
  • Benchmark contamination and inflated scores reduce differentiation between models; models can still hallucinate or behave inconsistently despite high published metrics, undermining their value for pricing risk.
  • Restricted-distribution frontier models (e.g., Anthropic’s Mythos) limit independent third-party evaluation; lack of access forces insurers to load for uncertainty rather than price actual risk, raising costs and slowing market development.
  • Gallagher Re urges evaluation approaches that use real-world inputs, adversarial testing and longitudinal monitoring to measure hallucination rates, decision consistency and correlated failure; insurers can use underwriting requirements, pricing signals and coverage design to incentivize transparency and robustness.
