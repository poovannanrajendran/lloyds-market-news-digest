US flood exposure points to large potential uninsured losses and credit pressure for local govs: Moody's Ratings - Reinsurance News

Source: www.reinsurancene.ws · Score: 0.80
Why it matters: Assesses uninsured flood losses and protection gaps with implications for insurance, credit risk, and resilience investments, relevant to the Lloyd's market.
  • Moody’s RMS-based assessment finds a large residential flood protection gap: under a 1-in-100-year event uninsured losses are about $375B (protection gap ~65%); a 1-in-500-year event could push uninsured losses above $1T (gap >70%).
  • Flood exposure is widespread but concentrated: ~90% of counties face some flood risk, most with potential uninsured losses ≤$150M, while high-loss counties (> $5B) are concentrated in Florida, Louisiana, South Carolina and Texas; a 1-in-500 event and midcentury climate scenario expand high-exposure counties into many additional states, including New Jersey by 2050.
  • Key drivers and caveats: rising frequency/severity of events, ongoing development in exposed areas and low insurance uptake drive growing credit/fiscal risk; Moody’s analysis assumes current insurance coverage and flood defences remain unchanged, so figures represent structural exposure rather than realised losses after future mitigation or policy changes.
  • Implications for insurers and public finance: significant and uneven fiscal exposure argues for higher insurance uptake, expanded risk transfer (private markets/reinsurance), revised underwriting/pricing and stronger resilience investment; state and local capacity to absorb shocks will hinge on reserves, federal support and governance frameworks.
