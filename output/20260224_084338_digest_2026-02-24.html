Source: www.artemis.bm · Score: 0.75

  • Global Parametrics' whitepaper positions catastrophe bonds (especially multi-year structures) and parametric products as useful components of humanitarian risk financing—able to provide larger limits and longer-term protection—but only as part of a broader, multi-instrument strategy.
  • Rising humanitarian needs and widening funding shortfalls are driving NGOs and donors toward market-based risk transfer; several humanitarian actors (Tearfund, UNICEF, IFRC, WFP) already use parametric insurance, while sovereign cat bonds have been issued in emerging markets.

Why it matters: Covers catastrophe bonds and parametric insurance—risk-transfer tools relevant to the Lloyd's market; discusses adoption, costs, and operational implications.
  • Cat bonds share parametric, hazard‑linked triggers with parametric insurance and provide access to capital markets, but are usually costlier than re/insurance for non‑peak perils and have so far been piloted mainly in sovereign contexts.
