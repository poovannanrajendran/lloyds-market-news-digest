Briefing: Confidence men and women – and the industry’s missing marketing narrative

Source: www.insurancetimes.co.uk · Score: 0.72
Why it matters: Discusses the insurance industry's marketing narrative and perception, directly relevant to the Lloyd's market
  • Insurance is a core social and economic enabler: by converting catastrophic risk into financeable and indemnifiable exposures, it underpins lending, trade, construction and personal recovery, enabling people and businesses to act with confidence.
  • There is an "insurance competence-salience paradox": when the industry does its job well the protection is invisible, which suppresses evidence of value and contributes to public indifference or suspicion.
  • Current marketing emphasises process, regulation and technicality (or resorts to fear-based messages and mascots), which frames insurance as a dull, grudging purchase rather than a purpose-driven enabler.
  • Practical recommendation for marketers: reframe narratives around enablement — promote how insurers make risk possible, support innovation and recovery, and largely honour their promises — to build greater public confidence and appreciation.
