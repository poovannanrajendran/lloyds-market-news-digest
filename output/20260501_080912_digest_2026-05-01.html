Source: www.businessinsurance.com · Score: 0.85

  • American Commercial Barge Line was identified as a potentially responsible party for contamination at a Jennings, LA shipyard tied to barges delivered for cleaning between 1965–1993; the court applied CERCLA’s strict liability and found the liability “arises out of” the predecessor’s ownership/arranging of the barges.

Mitigating M&A environmental risks with W&I and EIL

Source: www.globalreinsurance.com · Score: 0.80
Why it matters: Covers W&I and EIL insurance in M&A with Lloyd's market players and integrated risk transfer.
  • Environmental liabilities (historic and future) are a major M&A blind spot — contamination, remediation obligations, regulatory breaches and third‑party claims can surface post‑close and may attach to owners/operators regardless of fault, threatening deal value and certainty.
  • W&I vs EIL: W&I insurance protects contractual breaches of SPA representations but typically excludes or limits pollution exposures; EIL specifically underwrites pollution and environmental legal liabilities. Combining both covers SPA breaches plus known and unknown legacy and future environmental risks.
  • Early, coordinated insurer involvement is critical: underwriting W&I and EIL in parallel using the same due diligence and draft SPA (ideally with the same insurer) aligns policy terms with contract wording, reduces gaps/overlaps and accelerates transaction timetables.
  • Market trend: demand for integrated W&I+EIL solutions is rising across sectors beyond heavy industry (renewables, power, commercial real estate, infrastructure, supply chains) as ESG scrutiny, evolving regulation and redevelopment exposures increase environmental risk profiles.
