Smucker wins salmonella coverage fight - Business Insurance

Source: www.businessinsurance.com · Score: 0.72
Why it matters: Covers an insurance coverage dispute and policy interpretation in a product recall, with implications for risk transfer in the Lloyd's market.
  • Sixth Circuit ruled the 2022 J.M. Smucker salmonella peanut-butter outbreak constituted a single “occurrence,” so Smucker was required to meet only a $250,000 retention under each of its 2021 and 2022 CGL policies, rejecting Ace’s attempt to treat 225 production lots as separate occurrences and seek roughly $112.5 million in pre-coverage exposure.
  • The court relied on the policies’ definition of “occurrence” — covering an accident and any “continuous or repeated exposure to substantially the same general harmful conditions” — and applied Ohio’s cause test, looking to the insured’s perspective and the common production-contamination cause rather than the number of individual claimants.
  • A lot-based aggregation endorsement was held ambiguous because it never explicitly redefined “occurrence”; under Ohio law ambiguities are construed against the drafter (insurer). The panel also cited the ConAgra contaminated-peanut-butter precedent.
  • Practical implications for insurers and risk managers: draft clear, explicit aggregation/lot endorsements and occurrence definitions; anticipate courts may treat contamination/recall events as single occurrences based on common cause; and expect contra proferentem to be applied where endorsements are ambiguous.
