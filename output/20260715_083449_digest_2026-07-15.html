Missed e-filing deadline dooms worker’s retaliation suit - Business Insurance

Source: www.businessinsurance.com · Score: 0.82
Why it matters: Texas appeals court ruling on e-filing deadlines affecting a workers' compensation retaliation suit; implications for claims timing and coverage.
  • Texas Court of Appeals affirmed dismissal in Benavidez v. Cascade‑Nueces et al. because the plaintiff missed a court clerk’s deadline to correct an improperly formatted e‑filing and thus forfeited the original (timely) filing date, rendering the suit time‑barred.
  • Plaintiff transmitted her petition April 9, 2024 (within the two‑year limitations window) but the clerk returned it for an incomplete service request and combined documents; the clerk gave a deadline to correct (April 18) but the petition was not resubmitted until May 20, making the effective filing date after the limitations period.
  • The court reiterated that while Texas e‑filing rules treat transmission to the electronic filing service provider as filing, parties must meet a clerk’s correction deadline to retain the original filing date — missing that deadline can be dispositive.
  • Practical implications for insurers and defense counsel: strict attention to e‑filing form/compliance and statute‑of‑limitations timing is essential; procedural lapses can defeat otherwise viable claims, and defendants should preserve alternative substantive defenses (e.g., non‑subscription to workers’ comp).
