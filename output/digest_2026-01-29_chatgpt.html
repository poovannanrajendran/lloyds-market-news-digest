Executive summary

The specialty cycle demands decisive repositioning: heightened geopolitical and terrorism risk, accelerating cyber/AI threats and reinsurer willingness to deploy capital amid cedant cost pressure are reshaping capacity, terms and attachment strategies. Key themes

  • Terrorism and political violence risk persistence; public-private reinsurance mechanisms (Pool Re) remain critical
  • Renewal cycle: capital availability vs cedant cost-sensitivity — pricing replaced by terms and capacity allocation
  • Regional expansion in political risk (Middle East) via insurer-parent partnerships driving product availability
  • Opportunities for brokers and syndicates to reframe placement strategies — attachments, layered structures, parametric/top-up products
  • Placement platforms and cross-border branches as enablers of efficient capacity aggregation and regulatory-compliant transfers
  • Distribution consolidation and broker leverage
