  • July 17, 2026 M7.3 offshore quake was a near‑miss for the IBRD CAR Mexico 2024 Class B earthquake tranche — about 0.1 magnitude short of the M7.4 threshold that would have activated a 25% principal payout on the $70m Class B notes.
  • Transaction structure and exposures: the World Bank‑facilitated IBRD CAR Mexico 2024 package provides ~$420m of parametric protection (two earthquake tranches and one Atlantic hurricane tranche), with an additional $175m Pacific storm tranche issued separately; Class A = $225m (modelled EL ~0.9%, much higher trigger ~M7.9), Class B = $70m (modelled EL ~5.84%, lower trigger ~M7.4); coverage runs through April 2028.

  • Implications for insurers/ILS: highlights how parametric ‘box’ designs can be extremely sensitive to small magnitude differences, the importance of rapid event analysis and investor risk‑management workflows after events, and the value of Mexico’s diversified risk‑transfer program in containing fiscal exposure.

  • Kentucky Court of Appeals tossed a nearly $164 million jury verdict holding GEICO vicariously liable for a subcontracted tow-truck driver who rear‑ended a stopped vehicle in 2017, ruling the insurer cannot be held liable for the independent contractor’s negligence.
  • The court found the driver’s inattentive, high‑speed rear‑end crash created a “new risk” not inherent to towing, rejecting the trial court’s application of an “inherently dangerous” exception and declining to apply a heavy‑truck statute to justify vicarious liability.
