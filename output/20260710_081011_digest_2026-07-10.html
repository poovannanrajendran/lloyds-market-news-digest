School district fails to establish comp exclusivity defense in sex abuse case - Business Insurance

Source: www.businessinsurance.com · Score: 0.72
Why it matters: Covers workers' compensation coverage and exclusivity issues in a liability/claims context, relevant to insurance market risk and coverage decisions.
  • NY Appellate Division, Second Dept. (Case No. 2025-08146) refused to dismiss Adult Survivors Act sexual‑abuse claims against the White Plains school district and board on workers' compensation exclusivity grounds because the defendants failed to prove they maintained workers' compensation coverage during the relevant period or that the employee received comp benefits; an affirmation about coverage was submitted for the first time in reply and was ineffective.
  • The court dismissed portions of negligence and negligent hiring/retention/supervision claims tied to conduct before the employee disclosed the abuse, finding the district established it lacked actual or constructive notice and the plaintiff failed to raise a triable issue on those pre‑disclosure acts.
  • Claims based on alleged conduct after the disclosure survived summary dismissal because the plaintiff raised a factual dispute about the district's knowledge; separately, vicarious liability claims, a negligent infliction of emotional distress claim, and a punitive damages demand against the board were dismissed.
  • Practical implications for insurers and risk managers: contemporaneous proof of workers' compensation coverage and documentation of benefit payments are critical to support exclusivity defenses; avoid relying on late affidavits; preserve records of notice and internal investigations, and focus defense strategy on post‑disclosure conduct where exposure is more likely to survive challenge.
