PRA drops planned London market reporting reforms

Source: www.insurancetimes.co.uk · Score: 0.84
Why it matters: Regulatory update affecting London market insurers/branches; mentions IUA and competitiveness of the London market.
  • PRA has dropped planned changes to the regulation of third‑country branches in the London market, reversing updates to the modification‑by‑consent process that would have moved larger branches onto a full (quarterly) reporting schedule.
  • The reversal follows industry criticism—most prominently from the International Underwriting Association (IUA)—which warned the proposed changes would increase costs and reporting burden for affected firms.
  • An internal PRA analysis found quarterly reporting from larger branches provides limited additional supervisory value relative to the costs, prompting the decision to abandon the proposals.
  • The IUA welcomed the outcome as evidence of effective regulator–industry consultation, saying the decision helps preserve a proportionate supervisory regime and maintains the competitiveness and attractiveness of the London market for international insurers.
