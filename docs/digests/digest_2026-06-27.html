WealthW-Group (Clone of FCA authorised firm)

Source: fca.org.uk
Why it matters: Clone firms impersonating FCA-authorised entities present acute reputational, financial and operational risk to brokers, syndicates and placement platforms: clients and counterparties can be misled into sending premiums or confidential information to fraudsters, undermining placements and client trust.
  • Embed routine FCA register verification into onboarding and renewal workflows; require screenshot or permalink evidence of registration for any counterparty claiming UK authorisation.
  • Implement payment-rail controls: require broker and insured confirmation protocols, two-factor verification for premium transfers and use of escrow/segregated client accounts for new payees.
  • Market communication: circulate targeted warnings to distribution networks and insureds, record and escalate reported clone attempts to FCA and to the market’s compliance forums to limit contagion.

FXprobot / fxprobot.com

Source: fca.org.uk
Why it matters: Unauthorised entities offering financial services (eg. trading or premium financing solutions) create exposure to payment fraud, non-recoverable client money and loss of regulatory protections (FOS/FSCS). Brokers and platforms that interact with or promote such providers risk operational loss and regulatory scrutiny.
  • Restrict third-party financial services and fintech integrations to counterparties that are FCA‑authorised; maintain an approved vendor register and require annual revalidation of status.
  • Contractual and indemnity hygiene: insert clear representations on regulatory status, audit rights and indemnities when engaging payment or trading vendors to protect syndicates and MGAs.
  • Operational monitoring: deploy real‑time transaction analytics and alerts for anomalous payment destinations and unusual trading flows; escalate suspicious activity for immediate payment freezes and investigation.
