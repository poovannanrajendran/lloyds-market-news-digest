Executive summary

Recent FCA notices highlight ongoing risks from unauthorised financial firms, a high-profile crypto/e-money provider exiting the UK, and the FCA-led process to establish a Future Entity for open banking standards. For Lloyd's market participants—brokers, syndicates, and placement platforms—these developments underscore heightened exposure to fraud, payment and custody disruption, AML/KYC gaps and operational dependencies on third-party platforms and rails. Executives should prioritise…

Key themes

  • Unauthorised firms and fraud targeting distribution channels and retail clients
  • Payment and custody disruption from crypto/e-money providers exiting the UK
  • Operational resilience and third-party dependency for placement platforms and brokers
  • AML/KYC exposure and client protection gaps (FSCS / Financial Ombudsman absence)
  • Strategic engagement with open-banking standards and API governance
  • Regional consolidation and single-license strategies in APAC

Foreverfxtrading

Source: fca.org.uk
Why it matters: FCA warning on an unauthorised entity (Foreverfxtrading) signals persistent risk of fraud and impersonation that can affect brokers, MGAs and placement platforms through client phishing, premium diversion, and reputational contagion if clients mistake unauthorised suppliers for market participants.
  • Risk: Increased threat of premium diversion and client asset loss where unauthorised firms impersonate brokers or use similar branding — direct vulnerability for distribution chains and intermediated placements.
  • Immediate action: Validate counterparty authorisation and domain/brand integrity across client-facing channels; publish clear client guidance on how to verify authorised brokers and payment instructions.
  • Strategic: Embed contractual controls and operational checks in placement workflows (dual confirmation of payment instructions, dedicated treasury controls) and train front-line staff to detect and escalate suspected impersonation attempts.

Bitgerms / bitgerms.com

Source: fca.org.uk
Why it matters: FCA warning on Bitgerms highlights the breadth of unauthorised crypto/FX and fintech-themed scams that intersect with specialty insurance when insureds or brokers engage digital counterparties for payments, collateral or product distribution.
  • Risk: Exposure to non‑recoverable losses where clients or brokers transfer premiums or collateral to unauthorised fintech/crypto firms absent FSCS or Ombudsman protection.
  • Immediate action: Require enhanced due diligence for any fintech or crypto counterparty used in placements, and mandate use of approved bank accounts/payment rails in binder and slip wording.
  • Strategic: Review platform onboarding policies to ensure only authorised PSPs/e-money institutions are permitted; consider insurance wording amendments to exclude coverage where funds were sent to unauthorised firms.

Source: fca.org.uk

  • Strategic: Reassess acceptance of crypto-linked collateral and e‑money providers in Lloyd’s syndicate and broker policies; update contract clauses to address provider exits and require tiered contingency arrangements.
