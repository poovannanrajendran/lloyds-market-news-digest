RAYINVEST EDGE / RAYINVESTEDGE.COM

Source: fca.org.uk
Why it matters: FCA warning on RAYINVEST EDGE highlights the persistent risk of unauthorised firms targeting UK customers with financial services propositions. For Lloyd’s and global specialty brokers this creates the potential for contaminated risks, misdirected premiums and downstream complaints that can hit syndicate records and platform integrity.
  • Immediate: Instruct distribution teams and placement platforms to block, flag and escalate any inbound leads referencing this entity; run historical matching on recent new business and premium flows for suspect origin.
  • Due diligence: Require strict verification of intermediary authorisation (FCA register checks, corporate identity validation) before accepting new counterparties or onboarding via electronic placement platforms.
  • Contractual/procedural: Amend placement and broker agreements to include warranties on origin of business, anti‑fraud indemnities and rapid remediation clauses; coordinate with Compliance to file SARs and report to the FCA where appropriate.

TP24GLOBAL

Source: fca.org.uk
Why it matters: TP24GLOBAL appears on the FCA warning list as an unauthorised promoter. Such actors can create exposure through mis-sold cover, premium diversion, or impersonation of legitimate brokers — challenges that directly affect syndicate underwriting quality and platform trustworthiness.
  • Distribution hygiene: Mandate verified broker-of-record documentation for all new placements and require dual confirmation for premium remittance instructions to prevent diversion.
  • Platform controls: Ensure electronic placement platforms implement origin authentication (e.g., verified emails, API keys, audit trails) and automated blocking of known fraudulent identifiers.
  • Risk remediation: Run an audit of recent claims and new business associated with channels likely to have been targeted; prepare a client-communications playbook and remediation budget should affected policyholders surface.

AssetRise International Bank (AIB)

Source: fca.org.uk
Why it matters: AssetRise International Bank (AIB) being flagged as unauthorised underscores premium‑financing and third‑party payment vulnerabilities. Fraudulent 'bank' fronts can be used to accept premiums, launder funds, or mislead policyholders about solvency — creating contingent liabilities for underwriters and brokers.
  • Premium finance verification: Strengthen KYC for finance providers linked to policies; require confirmation of authorised banking partners and validate payment rails before policy inception.
  • Escrow and custodial controls: Move high‑value or cross‑border premium flows into approved escrow arrangements or trustee accounts controlled by regulated entities to limit diversion risk.
  • Exposure review: Instruct syndicate risk teams to identify exposures tied to premium financing schemes and stress-test recoverability and capital impact; coordinate with Legal on recovery rights and disclosure obligations.

FCA to review claims management practices

Source: fca.org.uk
Why it matters: The FCA’s review of claims management practices signals increased focus on CMCs, law firms and intermediaries that influence claims volume, marketing conduct and customer outcomes. For Lloyd’s and specialty insurers, this may translate into heightened regulatory engagement, changes to claims handling standards, and upward pressure on claims costs and complaints trends.
  • Claims governance: Review and tighten contracts with claims management firms and panel solicitors — introduce KPIs for timeliness, transparency of referral sources, and clawback provisions for inappropriate referrals.
  • Operational readiness: Audit claims intake and marketing channels (including social media) for potential misleading practices; implement enhanced verification and customer‑consent capture to reduce duplicate sign-ups and spurious claims.
  • Regulatory strategy: Prepare stakeholder engagement materials (impact assessments, remediation plans) for the FCA; consider industry coordination via market associations to define consistent standards and protect syndicate capital.
