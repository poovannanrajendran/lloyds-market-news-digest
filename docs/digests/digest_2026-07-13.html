Compare My Insurance trains and recruits every eight weeks to boost talent pipeline

Source: insurancetimes.co.uk
Why it matters: Compare My Insurance’s academy model demonstrates a repeatable, high-frequency recruitment and training pipeline that reduces dependence on a limited pool of experienced brokers. For Lloyd’s, syndicates and global specialty brokers this model presents both an operational opportunity to secure distribution talent and a strategic risk if new hires are not trained to specialty standards. Placement platforms and managing agents should view broker academies as potential partners or channels to standardize competencies, shorten time-to-placement and protect underwriting quality across complex classes.
  • Strategic partnerships: Managing agents and syndicates should explore formal partnerships with broker academies to co-design specialty curricula, fast-track authorizations and align candidate competencies with Lloyd’s slip and binding requirements.
  • Operational efficiency: Placement platforms must integrate training modules and simulated placement workflows to accelerate new-broker productivity, reduce slip rework and maintain placement velocity for time-sensitive specialty risks.
  • Quality and retention: Brokers should combine technical specialty training, mentorship with senior underwriters, and measurable competency KPIs to ensure new cohorts convert into sustainable, high-quality distribution resources rather than short-term hiring throughput
