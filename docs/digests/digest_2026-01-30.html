Executive summary

Market signals from recent Artemis coverage indicate continued expansion and maturation of the ILS ecosystem, rising institutional investor participation, and structural shifts that matter directly to Lloyd's syndicates, global specialty carriers, brokers and placement platforms. While public cat bond issuance and private ILS growth sustain alternative capacity inflows, traditional reinsurance pricing is softening into upcoming renewals. Strategic implications center on preserving underwriting…

Key themes

  • Insurance‑linked securities (ILS) growth and yield compression
  • Private ILS and bespoke collateralised reinsurance opportunity
  • Third‑party capital as permanent structural capacity
  • Reinsurance price softening and renewal cycle implications
  • Attachment points, portfolio quality and underwriting discipline
  • Talent and placement platform expansion in Bermuda and specialty markets

Source: fca.org.uk

  • Strategic: incorporate FCA Warning List checks into standard onboarding and ongoing vendor monitoring for brokers and placement platforms; escalate repeat-name hits to compliance and market associations.
