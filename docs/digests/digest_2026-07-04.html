Global Trade Express / globaltradeexpresslimited.com

Source: fca.org.uk
Why it matters: FCA warning on Global Trade Express highlights the risk of unauthorised entities posing as insurers, brokers or intermediaries and creating exposure for brokers, syndicates and placement platforms through misdirected premiums, fraudulent binders and loss of regulatory protections.
  • Verify FCA authorisation status before onboarding counterparties and before accepting new client payment instructions; require evidence of regulatory permissions for UK risk placement.
  • Tighten premium remittance controls: enforce named trust accounts, dual-authorisation for transfers, and reconciliation processes to prevent misdirected funds to unauthorised firms.
  • Update placement platform and broker client alerts and training so front-office staff can identify impersonation attempts, suspicious domains and requests that fall outside agreed onboarding protocols.

Global Crest Markets

Source: fca.org.uk
Why it matters: Global Crest Markets FCA notice is another signal that fraudsters exploit brand and market complexity to solicit premiums and client funds; the Lloyd's ecosystem is vulnerable through intermediated flows, special-purpose vehicles and sidecars accepting capital or premiums.
