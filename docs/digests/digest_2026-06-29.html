Source: fca.org.uk
Why it matters: FCA warning about 'Wealth Gaf' indicates an unauthorised entity potentially targeting UK clients — a direct threat to placement integrity, client funds and the reputations of brokers and syndicates that may be exposed through indirect introductions or platform integrations.

NEXUS SECURE ASSETS

Source: fca.org.uk
Why it matters: FCA notice on 'NEXUS SECURE ASSETS' highlights persistent scam/unauthorised firm risk. For Lloyd's brokers and syndicates this amplifies counterparty, settlement and claims integrity risks when dealing through multiple intermediaries or white-label platforms.

  • Contractual and insurance protections: review fronting/reinsurance and broker agreements for clauses that mitigate exposure to losses arising from dealings with unauthorised parties, including audit rights and indemnities.

Non-UK business removed from Consumer Duty scope to reduce burdens on wholesale businesses

Source: fca.org.uk
Why it matters: FCA proposals removing genuinely non-UK business from Consumer Duty scope reduce compliance burdens for wholesale operations but require Lloyd's market participants to remap distribution chains, verify 'non-UK' status and document governance to avoid inadvertent extension of UK protections.
  • Scope mapping and policy update: conduct a market-wide exercise to map customer journeys and determine where Consumer Duty still applies; update compliance manuals, placement platform rules and outsourcing arrangements accordingly.
  • Contractual allocation of responsibilities: renegotiate or clarify responsibilities across brokers, MGAs, platforms and syndicates for product design, disclosure and monitoring where flows are out-of-scope to ensure consistent standards and liability allocation.
  • Platform and product design controls: update placement platform logic to tag and segregate genuinely non-UK flows, implement controls to prevent mis-routing into UK-protected channels, and maintain auditable evidence supporting out-of-scope determinations.
