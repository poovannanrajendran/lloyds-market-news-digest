COREVEST / corevests.com

Source: fca.org.uk
Why it matters: FCA warning on COREVEST signals ongoing risk from unauthorised firms targeting UK clients; for Lloyd’s market participants this raises placement, client‑money and reputational exposure when intermediaries or platforms are used without robust verification.
  • Immediate: enforce a mandatory FCA‑authorisation check and documentary evidence requirement for any new intermediary or platform used to place or distribute risk.
  • Controls: update broker onboarding and review processes to include real‑time sanction and warning‑list screening, and escalate suspicious findings to compliance and legal teams.
  • Market actions: communicate clear guidance to syndicates and underwriters about rejecting business routed via unauthorised firms and preserving contractual remedies where placement involved questionable counterparties.

DRAGOFUND / dragofund.com

Source: fca.org.uk
Why it matters: DRAGOFUND warning underscores persistent investor/insurance product scams that can indirectly expose brokers, placement platforms and syndicates to fraudulent flows, payment disputes and claims of mis‑selling or distribution failings.
  • Due diligence: require enhanced KYC and economic‑substance checks for partners offering investment‑linked products or aggregator services before any referral or placement activity.
  • Client protection: ensure clear disclosure protocols and client suitability assessments are documented and retained to defend against complaints or Financial Ombudsman enquiries where unauthorised activity is alleged.
  • Remediation planning: maintain a defined escalation path with compliance, client‑money controls and legal to freeze or unwind affected placements and coordinate notification to regulators where exposures materialise.

Market Options

Source: fca.org.uk
Why it matters: Market Options appears on the FCA warning list, reinforcing that even seemingly market‑facing platforms may be unauthorised; this threatens platform interoperability, premium flows and settlement integrity for Lloyd’s brokers and syndicates.
  • Platform governance: impose pre‑launch acceptance criteria for third‑party platforms, covering FCA status, operational resilience, escrow arrangements and audit rights for insurers and managing agents.
  • Transaction controls: require segregation of funds, reconciliation evidence and contractually mandated audit access where premiums or client monies transit unauthorised or new platforms.
  • Strategic liaison: engage with market bodies (eg LMA) to create a shared registry of approved placement platforms and recommended minimum controls to limit systemic distribution risk.

FCA, Bank of England and Treasury joint statement on frontier AI models and cyber resilience

Source: fca.org.uk
Why it matters: The joint FCA/Bank of England/Treasury statement on frontier AI and cyber resilience signals that AI‑driven cyber capabilities materially elevate threat velocity and sophistication; Lloyd’s market participants face amplified operational, underwriting and third‑party risks if controls lag.
  • Resilience investment: accelerate testing of cyber fundamentals (patching, segmentation, access controls) and introduce adversarial‑AI scenario exercises into tabletop incident response plans at board and senior management level.
  • Third‑party risk: expand vendor risk frameworks to cover AI model use by brokers, MGAs, placement platforms and data providers, including contractual obligations on model provenance, testing and rapid mitigation.
  • Underwriting and exposure: review policy wordings, catastrophe modelling and aggregation analyses for cyber and AI‑related events, and consider mandatory cyber hygiene endorsements or premium adjustments where platform or intermediary risks are elevated.
