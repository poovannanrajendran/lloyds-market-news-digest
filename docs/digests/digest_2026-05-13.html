Highlights

Coinvexra

Source: fca.org.uk
Why it matters: FCA warning on Coinvexra signals active attempts by unauthorised firms to target UK customers and, by extension, international placement channels. For Lloyd’s and specialty markets this represents an elevated fraud vector against brokers, digital platforms and syndicates that rely on third‑party introducers or new digital distribution partners.
  • Embed FCA warning‑feed and automated register checks into broker/platform onboarding to block known unauthorised entities at first contact.
  • Require proof of authorisation, FSCS/ombudsman status where relevant, and validated corporate identity before accepting introductions or routing capacity.
  • Enhance transaction and payment controls for new counterparties (delayed settlement, source‑of‑fund checks, minimum manual review thresholds) to reduce exposure to scams.

Quanttekel.com

Source: fca.org.uk
Why it matters: The Quanttekel.com notice repeats the pattern of unauthorised actors operating under the radar. This reinforces the need for continuous monitoring and stricter front‑door controls on placement platforms, broker panels and MGA onboarding processes used by Lloyd’s syndicates.
  • Operationalise continuous screening of introducers against FCA warning lists and international enforcement repositories as part of tranche‑one onboarding.
  • Force multilayer authentication (regulated status, beneficial ownership, digital footprint corroboration) before granting API or electronic placement access.
  • Strengthen contractual indemnities and onboarding warranties from brokers and MGAs to mitigate recovery risk where business flows from unauthorised sources.

FCA fines and bans Frank Breuer for serious misconduct in pension transfer advice

Source: fca.org.uk
Why it matters: The FCA fine and ban of Frank Breuer for repeated misconduct and failure to maintain professional insurance highlights two critical exposures for the market: adviser/producer conduct risk and insurance protection gaps. Syndicates and brokers face potential claims, regulatory inquiries and client remediation obligations where intermediaries act without required PI cover or with compromised integrity.
  • Require documented evidence of appropriate PI and firm-level authorisations as a condition precedent to binding authority, referrals or delegated authority arrangements.
  • Introduce routine audits and spot checks of advisers and distribution partners specifically focused on customer outcomes, suitability assessments and insurance coverage status.
  • Establish escalation protocols and contingency plans (client remediation, claims notification to underwriters, regulatory reporting) where breaches of conduct or coverage are identified.
