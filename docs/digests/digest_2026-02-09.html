Executive summary

Recent developments signal continued maturation and layering of alternative capital across the global specialty reinsurance market. Continued use of catastrophe bonds by tech-enabled insurers, domicile-level regulatory easing for RSPVs, an observable shift toward institutional sophistication among ILS investors, and demonstrable sidecar returns all reinforce a durable convergence between capital markets and traditional Lloyd's syndicates. Strategic responses are required from managing agents,…

Key themes

  • Expansion of ILS and collateralised reinsurance capacity
  • Regulatory and accounting facilitation for RSPVs (Malta) enhancing domicile competition
  • Institutional sophistication and longer-term commitments from ILS investors
  • Sidecars and engineered capital delivering predictable income streams
  • Implications for Lloyd's syndicates, brokers and placement platforms — pricing, placement strategy and data transparency
  • Unauthorised intermediaries and impersonation risk
