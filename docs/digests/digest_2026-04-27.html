Consumers will pay to avoid AI in claims – and the youngest are most willing

Source: insurancetimes.co.uk
Why it matters: The finding that a majority of customers — notably younger cohorts — will pay to avoid automated claims processes is material for Lloyd’s syndicates, global specialty carriers and brokers because it challenges assumptions that AI-driven automation is universally value-creating. In specialty lines where complexity, speed of recovery, reputational risk and broker relationships matter, the willingness-to-pay for human adjudication affects product design, distribution economics, placement platform workflows and underwriting margin.
  • Commercialise choice: design products with tiered claims journeys (automated, assisted, fully human) and attach explicit premiums or service fees so underwriters and syndicates can monetise preference for human handling while protecting loss ratios.
  • Equip brokers as value creators: reposition brokers and coverholders to sell human-assisted claims as a differentiator on complex specialty placements, with training, scripted propositions and commission/fee models aligned to advisory outcomes.
  • Re-architect platforms and operations: implement hybrid claims orchestration in placement and claims platforms — selective automation with human handoffs, clear SLAs, audit trails and customer-choice flags — to mitigate regulatory and reputational risk while preserving efficiency.
